# Talking Points Guide — Green Screen Reactions

The green-screen deliverable is a **hook + talking points**, not a script. This is deliberate. Reaction
content lives or dies on sounding natural and current. A realtor reading a script to camera looks stiff
and dated; a realtor riffing from bullet points sounds like the local expert reacting in real time.

So: **the hook is written word-for-word** (it's the scroll-stopper — it must land exactly). **Everything
after the hook is bullet prompts** the agent expands in their own voice.

---

## The structure

### 1. Hook — word-for-word, ~12 words max (one breath)
One line, read exactly as written, from `hook-formulas.md`. It references the stat or the local market.
This is the only scripted part.

### 2. Talking points — 4–6 bullets, in this beat order
Each bullet is a **prompt, not a sentence to read**. Shorthand. The agent knows the topic; the bullet
just keeps them on track.

1. **The setup** — what happened / the key number. One beat. (e.g. "rates held a 3rd time — still 5.x%")
2–3. **The take** — what it actually means for a buyer/seller in [city] *right now*. This is where the
   value lives. Two beats. Opinionated and specific. (e.g. "buyers under $600K → expect competition
   back"; "this is NOT the time to lowball in the NW")
4. **Forward look / advice** — what the agent is telling clients. (e.g. "what I'm telling my buyers this
   week…")
5. **CTA** — one beat, matched to the funnel role (soft by default for green screen — Awareness/Authority).

### 3. On-screen text notes — 2–3 short overlays
Max ~6 words each. The key stat, the city, a key phrase. These also serve as the "background spec" if
the agent wants to build a graphic in claude.ai/design (we describe; we don't render).

---

## Rules that make it work

- **Opinionated.** Every package needs one real take — "here's what I'm telling my clients," "most
  people read this as bad news; I disagree." A neutral recap is not content.
- **Local.** Name the city/market. Tie the stat to a specific price band or neighbourhood when possible.
- **"You"-focused.** Talk to one buyer or seller, not an audience.
- **No news-anchor language.** Never "according to this article," "experts say," "a report found." The
  agent already knows this — they're sharing it, not reporting it.
- **Shorthand, not prose.** If a talking point reads like a full sentence to recite, cut it down. The
  test: could the agent glance at it and keep talking? If they'd have to *read* it, it's too long.
- **One stat minimum.** At least one specific number from the article in the talking points.

---

## Example (shape only)

**Article:** "Bank of Canada holds rate for third straight meeting" · key stat: overnight rate 2.75%

```
HOOK (word-for-word):
"The Bank of Canada just blinked. Here's what it means for Calgary."

TALKING POINTS (riff):
• Rate held a 3rd time — borrowing costs aren't climbing for now
• For Calgary buyers under $600K: this is your window before spring competition
• Sellers — buyers feel less rate panic, so realistic pricing moves fast right now
• What I'm telling my clients this week: get pre-approved now, lock your rate hold
• CTA: DM me "RATE" and I'll send you this week's Calgary numbers

ON-SCREEN TEXT: "Rate held: 2.75%" · "Calgary buyers" · "Your window"
Runtime: ~45 sec
```

Note the take beats carry the value; the hook is the only line read verbatim; nothing is a script.
