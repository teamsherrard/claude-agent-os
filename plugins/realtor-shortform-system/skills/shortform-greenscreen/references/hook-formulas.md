# Hook Formulas — Green Screen Reactions

## The Rule
For a daily green screen, write **one hook** — the best fit for today's article. Read it word-for-word.
Hooks are **~12 words max (one breath)**. Pick a formula that matches the article type (see the selection guide), and
vary the formula day to day so the agent's feed doesn't feel one-note (glance at `content-log.md`).

Per Mike's HVC framework (`${CLAUDE_PLUGIN_ROOT}/shared/mike-frameworks.md`): the hook has **~3 seconds**
to land, so make it a **bold or slightly controversial statement**. **NEVER tell people to "stop
scrolling" / "don't scroll"** — it reads as desperate. The hook earns attention by being interesting.

---

## 10 Hook Formulas

### 1. The Stat Lead
Lead with the number. The number does the work.
Template: "[City] [stat] just [changed]. Here's what that means."
Example: "Calgary inventory just dropped to its lowest point in 3 years."
Best for: Market stats, price changes, rate announcements, inventory reports.

### 2. The Contrarian Take
Challenge what the viewer assumes. Creates instant curiosity.
Template: "Everyone thinks [X]. The data says something different."
Example: "Everyone thinks this is a bad time to buy. The numbers disagree."
Best for: Market outlook, rate news, affordability stories where the real answer surprises people.

### 3. The Direct Address
Speak directly to one type of viewer. Makes them feel called out (in a good way).
Template: "If you're [specific viewer] in [city], you need to see this."
Example: "If you're selling in Calgary this spring, you need to see this."
Best for: Seller-specific news, buyer opportunity articles, market shift stories.

### 4. The Window
Frame the moment as an opportunity that won't last.
Template: "There's a window open right now in [city]. Here's what it is."
Example: "There's a window open for Calgary buyers that wasn't there last year."
Best for: Rate drops, inventory increases, softening markets — when conditions favour action.

### 5. The Myth Buster
Name a common belief and bust it with the article's finding.
Template: "Most [buyers/sellers] believe [X]. This data shows that's not true."
Example: "Most Calgary buyers think they need 20% down. They don't."
Best for: First-time buyer content, affordability stories, misconception-correcting articles.

### 6. The Local Lens
Put the city front and center. Hyper-local hooks outperform generic ones.
Template: "[City] just [specific event]. Here's what it actually means."
Example: "The Calgary board just released their numbers. One stat stood out."
Best for: Board stats releases, local policy news, city-specific updates.

### 7. The Before/After
Set up the contrast. What changed and why it matters.
Template: "[X] months ago [city] looked like [A]. Today it looks like [B]."
Example: "Six months ago Calgary had 3,000 listings. Today, under 1,800."
Best for: Year-over-year comparisons, market shift articles, trend stories.

### 8. The Question
A direct question that makes the viewer want the answer.
Template: "Are you [doing/thinking the thing] in [city]? Watch this first."
Example: "Are you waiting for rates to drop in Calgary? Watch this first."
Best for: Rate articles, timing-the-market content, decision-making stories.

### 9. The Honest Take
Signal that what follows is the agent's real opinion — not a sales pitch.
Template: "My honest take on [what just happened in city]."
Example: "My honest take on what the Bank of Canada just did."
Best for: Rate decisions, forecasts, policy changes — anywhere the interpretation is the value.

### 10. The Specific Number
Lead with a number too precise to ignore.
Template: "[Specific number] — that's [what it represents] in [city] right now."
Example: "18 days. That's how long the average Calgary home sits on market."
Best for: Days on market, specific price data, inventory numbers, affordability metrics.

## Hook Selection Guide

| Article Type | Best Hook Formulas |
|---|---|
| Board stats release | Stat Lead, Local Lens, Specific Number |
| Rate announcement | Honest Take, Question, Window |
| Inventory/supply news | Before/After, Window, Stat Lead |
| Buyer affordability | Myth Buster, Question, Direct Address |
| Market forecast | Contrarian Take, Honest Take, Before/After |
| Local development | Local Lens, Direct Address, Window |
| Seller market update | Direct Address, Local Lens, Stat Lead |

---

## Hook Quality Test
Before locking the hook, ask:
- 12 words or fewer — one breath? (If not, trim)
- Names the city or references the local market? (Preferred)
- Would someone mid-scroll stop to hear the rest? (If not, rewrite)
- Different from the formula used in the last day or two? (Check `content-log.md`)
- Avoids hype words — "insane," "game-changer," "unbelievable"? (Must be yes)
- No "stop scrolling" / "don't scroll"? (Must be yes)
