# Mike's Short-Form Frameworks — the methodology (source of truth)

Every short-form skill follows this. It is Mike Sherrard's short-form methodology, embedded verbatim in
intent. When a skill says "follow Mike's frameworks," it means this file. Where anything else in the
plugin disagrees with this, **this wins.**

> **Scope:** this file owns short-form *execution* (the 4-3-2-1 ratio, HVC, filming/editing, cadence). The
> agent's brand + niche *strategy* — the **A.G.E.N.T. Brand OS** and the niching beliefs — lives in the

> reach split" here is the same as the Brain's "20% niche / 80% general." If that mix changes, update both.

## 1. The 4-3-2-1 Content Ratio
The content mix, by share of what the agent posts:

| # | Share | Type | What it is |
|---|---|---|---|
| **4** | **40%** | **Brand awareness / reach** | Content that gets attention: trends, hooks, relatable POVs, storytelling, entertainment, big ideas, "stop the scroll" content. |
| **3** | **30%** | **Value / education** | Tips, how-tos, frameworks, mistakes to avoid, myth-busting, quick wins, industry insights. |
| **2** | **20%** | **Trust / connection** | Behind-the-scenes, founder/team content, client stories, social proof, values, opinions, community-building. |
| **1** | **10%** | **Conversion / selling** | Offers, CTAs, product/service explanations, launches, lead magnets, booking prompts. |

The agent thinks in formats; **you** keep this ratio balanced silently across their content. Most agents
over-index on selling and on agent-bait — keep ~70% of posts reach + value.

## 2. HVC — the video structure (Hook · Value · CTA)
Every short-form video is built on HVC:

- **Hook** — you have **~3 seconds** to capture attention. A **bold or controversial statement** that
  stops the scroll. **NEVER tell people to "stop scrolling" / "don't scroll"** — it reads as desperate.
  The hook does the work by being interesting, not by begging.
- **Value** — deliver **clean, structured talking points, usually in a list** ("3 things…", "here's how…").
- **CTA** — **it doesn't always have to sell.** It can be *follow for more tips*, *share this with someone
  who needs it*, *comment X*, or a lead-magnet/booking ask. **The CTA aligns with the video's format** and
  the funnel role (only the ~10% conversion posts make a hard ask).

## 3. Core beliefs (how the agent actually makes these)
- **Filming:** raw is good — the front-facing phone camera is fine, but it **must be vertical (9:16)**.
  Frame it so on-screen **text and captions have room and don't get covered** (don't fill the whole frame
  with your face; leave head-room and lower-third space).
- **Editing:** keep it simple; **b-roll is optional** (nice if an AI editor adds it automatically, not
  required). **Raw, genuine, authentic delivery often outperforms polished** — sometimes no editing beyond
  captions feels more relatable and less like an ad. **Captions are the ONLY mandatory edit on every video.**
- **Captions & hashtags:** **3–5 searchable hashtags — no more.** Captions are **optimized per platform**
  (each needs different formatting + keywords to be searchable and indexed).
- **Length:** **45–60 seconds** is the real-estate sweet spot (varies by format).

## 4. Coaching answers (advise exactly like this when the agent asks)

- **How often should I post?** Bare minimum **3×/week**; the goal is **daily posts + daily stories** to
  build momentum. Short-form is about being (and staying) **top of mind** — consistency gets you there.
  **In the beginning, chase VOLUME to get the reps in**; worry about CTAs/lead-gen once there's momentum
  and on-camera confidence.
- **One profile or two?** **One profile.** Blend personal + business so people get to know you — the goal
  is the **know-like-trust** factor, and personal posts are what make people connect emotionally.
- **Small market?** Talk about your **local market** (a bit more often than on YouTube) AND reference the
  **closest well-known large market** near you to capture its search traffic and pull it toward yours.
- **Specific niche?** Talk about the niche **and** include broader content so new people can find you (the
  80/20 reach split).
- **What content should I start with?** The 4-3-2-1 mix + **stories**. The best starters:
  personal stories about you · your passions/hobbies · **client success stories told as their journey (not
  "sold!" / "congrats to my buyer")** · **home tours** (great reach + easy engagement) · **"things to do in
  [city] this week"** (pulls in locals, not just agents) · **green screen** (local news, top lists, real
  estate news — best for massive attention + engagement).
- **How long should videos be?** Usually **45–60 seconds** performs best for real estate.
- **Best time to post?** In the beginning it barely matters — test logical times: **weekdays lunch +
  evenings; weekends 9am–5pm.** After ~a month, use the agent's own insights (or the connected tool's
  best-time data) to post when their audience is active.
- **How do I get LOCALS to follow me (not just other agents)?** Agents-only followings come from posting
  **agent-bait** (mostly home tours). To reach locals, lean into:
  - **Green screen** — local news, real estate news, "things to do," top lists (restaurants, events,
    cafés, beaches, hikes).
  - **Local lifestyle content** — you visiting new cafés/restaurants, favourite parks, highlighting local
    events. This is the difference between a following of agents and a following of buyers/sellers.
