# Content types — the recipe per video type

Match the edit to the kind of video. These are the common realtor types — pull the right structure.

## Market update / data (e.g. "Spokane market 2026")
- Hook: a number or a myth-bust ("Is the market crashing? Here's the real data").
- Structure: hook → current conditions → numbers by area → what it means → CTA.
- Edit: stat cards / count-ups on every figure, a contextual animation on trends (price drop → down arrow), chapters (long-form). Restrained.

## Listing / home tour (→ `edit-listing`)
- Property footage ONLY (own clips or listing photos) — never generic stock for the home.
- Structure: drone / exterior + address → room by room → yard → neighbourhood → book-a-showing CTA.
- Edit: smooth room-to-room transitions, address/price lower-thirds.

## Just-listed / just-sold (short)
- Hook: "Just listed in [neighbourhood]" + the standout feature.
- Fast cuts of the best shots + a price / beds-baths card + CTA.

## Neighbourhood / relocation (e.g. "Moving to [city]")
- Hook: "Thinking of moving to [city]? Here's what you need to know."
- Real-place b-roll (own footage / Street View), pros & cons, lifestyle, CTA.

## Tips / educational (short or long)
- Hook: a bold claim or "3 things…".
- Numbered structure, keyword pops on each point, punch-ins, CTA.

## Testimonial / client win
- Hook: the result ("We got them $40k under asking").
- Let the client / story carry it; subtle b-roll; warm music; CTA.

## Always
Open on the hook · clean audio (Studio Sound) · end on a complete thought + the agent's CTA · on-brand · Standard tier by default.
