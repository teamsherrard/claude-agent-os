# Music (bed + ducking)

A reel needs a music bed, always ducked under the voice. Long-form ships without a bed by default — add a subtle one only if the agent asks (it's not part of the long-form pass plan).

## Mood → bed (match the content)
- Market update / data / tips → light, modern, confident underscore (~90–110 BPM).
- Listing / luxury → refined, atmospheric, cinematic (~80–100 BPM).
- Hype / just-listed / promo → energetic, upbeat (~110–130 BPM).
- Story / client win / mindset → warm, inspirational cinematic (~60–90 BPM).
- Audience overrides genre — a luxury brand never gets EDM.

## Levels
- Duck the music well under speech (~-17 to -20 dB under the voice) so the speaker is always clearly on top.
- Use gentle sidechain ducking (music dips when he talks, lifts in the gaps / over b-roll).
- Fade in and out at the ends; never start or stop abruptly.

## Licensing
- Use Descript's licensed library (or the agent's own licensed tracks). Never random copyrighted music — it kills reach and risks takedowns.
