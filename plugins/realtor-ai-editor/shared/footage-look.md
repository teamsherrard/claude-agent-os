# Footage look & treating people well

How the footage — and the people in it — should look. Subtle and flattering always beats "punchy."

## Colour grade — subtle and natural only

- A gentle lift is fine: slight contrast, a touch of warmth, balanced brightness. It should look crisp, never "filtered."
- **Hard guardrails (these have gone wrong before):** never over-saturate, never over-brighten, never crush the blacks, never anything neon / harsh / pixelated. Skin tones stay natural and flattering — never orange, never washed out.
- Tiny tweaks only. When in doubt, do less — and offer to dial it back.

## Treat people flatteringly (this matters)

Many agents — **especially women in the cohort** — are self-conscious on camera about their face, skin, hair, and makeup. Default to making them look their best:

- **Gentle zooms only (~1.05–1.08×). NEVER a tight push-in on someone's face.**
- Keep the face well-framed with headroom; don't crop in close.
- Natural, flattering skin; never harsh brightness or contrast on a face.
- When unsure, prioritise making the person look good over a "punchier" edit.
- Never comment on or try to "fix" someone's appearance — just frame and grade them kindly.
