# Script Framework — High-Conversion (Real Estate)

The lead-generating structure for real-estate YouTube scripts. Use alongside `script-format.md`.

## The 4-part framework
1. **Hook (0–15s)** — grab attention + make a promise that matches the title/thumbnail. The first 15s decide whether they keep watching.
2. **Early Warm CTA (15–30s)** — ⭐ the lead-gen secret most agents miss. Right after the hook, a friendly,
   non-salesy, value-driven invitation to connect — it captures high-intent viewers BEFORE they drop off.
   Position yourself as a helpful resource, not a pitch.
3. **Main content (body)** — deliver on the hook's promise; organized, conversational, with pattern interrupts + stories.
4. **Final CTA & end screen** — for those who watched to the end: a clear, specific next step + a nudge to the end-screen elements.

## Hook types (pick one, match the title)
- **Bold statement:** "Most agents are lying to you about this market. Here's the truth."
- **Question:** "What does $700k actually buy you in south Calgary?"
- **Statistic:** "40% of people who move to [city] leave within 5 years — here's why."
- **In medias res:** open on the single best feature/moment, then "…let me show you the rest."
- **Preview:** a 3–5s montage of the best moments to come.

## Early Warm CTA — examples (friendly, low-pressure, with social proof)
- **Relocation:** "By the way — if you're thinking about a move to [city], we get calls and emails every
  single day from people just like you, and we love it. Whether you're moving in 9 days or 90, reach out."
- **Market update:** "Before the numbers — if you're a [city] homeowner curious what these changes mean
  for your home's value, there's a free, no-obligation home-value tool linked below."
- **First-time buyer:** "We're about to break down the whole process — but if you're feeling overwhelmed
  and just want to talk through your situation, there's a link below to book a free, no-pressure call."
> Tone: friendly, welcoming, never salesy. "We get calls from people just like you every day" is powerful
> social proof that normalizes reaching out. Always honor the agent's voice + hard-avoids.

## Keep them watching
- **Pattern interrupts every 30–60s:** on-screen text/graphics, B-roll cue, angle/zoom change, a sound cue.
- **Tell stories, not just facts** — a client who made that mistake; the family gatherings that kitchen could host.
- **Conversational tone** — write it the way the agent actually talks; it must sound natural read aloud.

## Final CTA
- Be specific: "If you're ready to start your search in [city], book a free consult — link below," not "contact me."
- Point to the end screen: "Want more neighbourhood tours? Click the playlist here. Not subscribed yet? Tap here."
