# Script Framework — High-Conversion (Real Estate)

The lead-generating structure for real-estate YouTube scripts. Use alongside `script-format.md`.
**This applies the YouTube Doctrine** (`${CLAUDE_PLUGIN_ROOT}/shared/youtube-doctrine.md`) — §4 structure,
§5 hooks, §6 CTAs (+§26 library), §7 body. The doctrine OVERRIDES anything generic below.

## The structure (doctrine §4)
1. **Hook (0–30/60s)** — grab attention + make a promise that matches the title/thumbnail. Follow the §5.4
   formula: call out the viewer/situation → introduce the tension/mistake/question/opportunity → say what the
   video helps them understand → add a reason to stay to the end. NEVER "welcome back," a long personal intro,
   "today we're going to talk about…," or a credentials dump (§5.5).
2. **Primary CTA (right after the hook)** — Mike's §6.2 structure: brief intro + "people just like you"
   social proof + speak to "your unique situation" + "avoid costly mistakes" + book a private call (link in
   description). This is the lead-gen move most agents miss — it captures high-intent viewers early. Use the
   §6.4 variation for the avatar and the §26 library; keep it short, warm, a helpful resource, not a pitch.
3. **Body** — deliver on the hook's promise (§7): logical order, plain language, local specificity, no random
   tangents; answer *what they need to know · why it matters · what to do* and translate facts into guidance
   (don't info-dump). Organized, conversational, with pattern interrupts + stories.
4. **Secondary CTA + next-video direction (the very end)** — the final thing said (§6.5): remind them to
   book/reach out + point to the **next best video** (general for a newer channel, a specific continuation for
   an established one). Then nudge the end-screen elements.

## Hook types (pick one, match the title — all obey §5)
- **Direct question:** "What does $700k actually buy you in south Calgary?"
- **Fear / mistake:** "Before you buy in [city], there are three mistakes that could cost you thousands."
- **Timely angle:** "The [city] market just shifted again — and it matters if you're buying or selling this summer."
- **Contrarian angle:** "Most people moving to [city] only look at the obvious areas — but the best options are just outside the city."
- **Clear promise:** "By the end, you'll know which areas to consider, which to be careful with, and how to avoid overpaying."

## Primary CTA — examples (right after the hook; §6.2 / §6.4 / §26)
- **General (§6.2):** "If you're new here, my name is [Name] with [Brokerage], and I have people just like
  you reaching out every week looking for guidance with their next move. If you'd like to chat about your
  unique situation and how I can help you avoid costly mistakes, click the link in the description and book a
  private call at your convenience."
- **Relocation (§6.4):** "If you're planning a move to [city], I help people just like you every week figure
  out where to live, what to avoid, and how to move with more confidence. Use the link in the description to book a private call."
- **Seller (§6.4):** "If you're thinking about selling in [city] and want to know what your home could
  realistically sell for and how to avoid leaving money on the table, use the link in the description to book a private call."
> Tone: friendly, welcoming, never salesy. "People just like you" is powerful social proof that normalizes
> reaching out; "your unique situation" + "avoid costly mistakes" resonate (§6.3). Always honor the agent's
> voice + hard-avoids, and tie the CTA to the agent's real booking link / lead magnet from the Brain.

## Keep them watching
- **Pattern interrupts every 30–60s:** on-screen text/graphics, B-roll cue, angle/zoom change, a sound cue.
- **Tell stories, not just facts** — a client who made that mistake; the family gatherings that kitchen could host.
- **Conversational tone** — write it the way the agent actually talks; it must sound natural read aloud.

## Secondary CTA (the very end — §6.5)
Two components, and it's the last thing said:
- **Book / reach out**, specific: "If you're ready to start your search in [city], book a free consult — link below," not "contact me."
- **Point to the next best video:** newer channel → general ("watch this next where I break down what you need
  to know before relocating to [city]"); established channel → a specific continuation ("the next video you
  should watch is my breakdown of the best neighbourhoods in [city]"). Then nudge the end screen / playlist / subscribe.
