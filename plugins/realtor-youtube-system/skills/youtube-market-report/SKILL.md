# Monthly Market Report

Most agents struggle to make market updates — yet they drive serious authority and seller leads. This makes
it nearly effortless: **we build the deck, they hit record.** Apply `${CLAUDE_PLUGIN_ROOT}/shared/house-rules.md`.

> **Applies the YouTube Doctrine** (`${CLAUDE_PLUGIN_ROOT}/shared/youtube-doctrine.md`) — **§9 Market Updates**:
> structure (§9.1), **timing (§9.3)**, **interpret-don't-recap (§9.4)**, common mistakes (§9.7), and the
> **Claude output standard (§9.8)**. Market updates are a core pillar (§8) and one of the strongest authority +
> seller-lead engines. This is a **flagship deliverable** → it keeps the credibility stamp (house rule #9).

## Step 1 — Pull LAST month's complete LOCAL data, title it THIS month (sourced, never invented)
**Timing (§9.3):** record in the **first week of the month**, using the **previous month's COMPLETE data** (a
full month of stats), but put the **CURRENT month in the title** so it matches what viewers are searching now.
(e.g. recorded first week of June, on May's data, titled "… June [Year]".) Use the Research Engine method
(`${CLAUDE_PLUGIN_ROOT}/skills/youtube-research/references/research-method.md`) scoped to the agent's market:
benchmark/median price, sales volume, inventory / months of supply, days on market, YoY changes, current
rates — every figure with **source + date**. Niche extras where relevant (e.g., new-construction starts/
incentives). If a number can't be verified, leave it out — never guess on camera.

## Step 2 — Build the presentation (the part agents can't do themselves)
A clean, simple deck (~8–12 slides) the agent records over, following the §9.1 market-update structure (hook →
primary CTA → data → interpretation → buyer/seller guidance → secondary CTA). Use the available presentation
tooling (.pptx), simple and uncluttered — brand colours from the Brain if available. One idea per slide, big
numbers. **Interpret, don't recap (§9.4):** every stat slide says what the number *means* for the viewer — is
inventory rising or falling, are prices firming or softening, are homes selling faster or slower, who's gaining
leverage — never a bare data read-out (§9.7 common mistakes: stats without interpretation, stale data,
unsupported predictions, overhyping, fear without guidance, ignoring price-point/property-type differences,
talking to agents instead of consumers, forgetting the CTA).
1. **Title** — "[City] Market Update — [Month Year]" + agent name (CURRENT month per §9.3).
2. **The headline** — the one-line story of the month.
3. **3–4 stat slides** — one number each (price · sales · inventory · rates), each with a plain-English line
   on **what it means** (not just the figure).
4. **What this means for BUYERS** — 2–3 takeaways for local buyers.
5. **What this means for SELLERS** — 2–3 takeaways (pricing, prep, timing).
6. **What this means if you're MOVING HERE** — the relocation lens.
7. **What I'd do this month** — the agent's grounded, no-guarantee guidance.
8. **CTA** — the agent's real CTA + booking link.

## Step 3 — Talking points (so they can just hit record)
Under every slide, write 2–4 **conversational talking-point lines in the agent's voice** — not an essay, just
what to say while that slide is up. The agent opens the deck, starts a Loom/screen-share, and talks through
it. **No editing, no memorizing, no teleprompter.**

## Step 4 — Round out the §9.8 package + save
Alongside the deck + talking points, give the agent the rest of the **§9.8 market-update output standard** so
the video is ready to film and publish: **search-intent title options** (use the §9.5 market-update title
patterns — city + CURRENT month + a clear payoff), **thumbnail text** (3–5 words; talking-head/market-update
thumbnail = headshot ≈ 33% + expression, doctrine §17.2), a **hook** (§9.6 — open on the shift, not "welcome
back"), the **primary CTA**, **buyer takeaways**, **seller takeaways**, a **closing CTA**, a **description
opening** (CTA + booking link first, §18.2), and a **suggested next video** to promote.
Save the deck + a talking-points doc into `Videos/{YYYY-MM · Month}/{YYYY-MM-DD · Market Update}/`.
Then offer the usual: full SEO package · lead magnet (the report itself makes a great one — "get next month's
report by email") · repurposing (the deck slides double as an email + carousel source).

## Rules
- Sourced facts only; flag anything stale (§9.3 — don't use stale data). **No forecasts or guarantees** —
  compliance applies fully.
- **Interpret, never recap (§9.4):** the value is in what the numbers mean for buyers and sellers, not the
  numbers themselves.
- Three audience lenses (buyers / sellers / relocators) are mandatory — that's what makes it a lead engine.
- Keep the deck SIMPLE. Big numbers, short lines. It's a recording aid, not a design piece.
- **Flagship doc → keeps the credibility stamp** (house rule #9): byline under the title + footer credit
  `Powered by Mike Sherrard Coaching Inc Frameworks`. Keep it as a byline/footer only — never inside copy the
  agent publishes (title, description, script line).
