# Report Guide — Market Update Report Structure and Writing

## Purpose of the Report

This report does two jobs simultaneously:

1. **Screen share asset** — the agent shares it on screen during their YouTube recording.
   Viewers see the data while the agent narrates. It makes the video look professional and
   data-driven without the agent needing to build slides or graphics.

2. **Email distribution asset** — sent to the agent's list as a standalone PDF or attached
   document. Subscribers who don't watch the video still get the full market update in a
   readable, shareable format.

It must be clean enough to share publicly and specific enough to be genuinely useful.
Anyone reading it without context should understand what's happening in the market.

---

## Writing Tone for the Report

The report is written for buyers, sellers, and homeowners — not industry professionals.

**Always:**
- Use plain language. "Home prices rose 4%" not "benchmark price appreciation of 4% MoM"
- Give context for every number. "18 days on market — that means homes are selling fast"
- Explain what each metric means for the reader's decision
- Use the city/community name specifically — not "locally" or "in the area"

**Never:**
- Industry jargon without explanation
- Data without interpretation
- Generic commentary that could apply to any market anywhere

## Page-by-Page Writing Guide

### Page 1 — Cover
Simple. Clean. Professional.
- Title: "[City] Real Estate Market Update — [Month Year]"
- Agent name and photo placeholder
- Brokerage name (if applicable)
- Date prepared
- Agent's booking link / website

### Page 2 — Market at a Glance
This is the most important page. It's what the agent shows first on camera and what most
email readers will spend the most time on.

Layout: 8 large stat boxes in a 2×4 or 4×2 grid. Each box contains:
- The metric name (small, above)
- The number (large and bold, center)
- The YoY change (below, with ↑ or ↓ arrow and % or number)
- A one-line plain-English label (e.g., "Homes are selling fast" under DOM)

**YoY arrow color convention (describe in doc, designer applies):**
- Price up ↑ → Green for sellers, context for buyers
- Sales up ↑ → Green (active market)
- Inventory down ↓ → Red for buyers (less choice), green for sellers
- DOM down ↓ → Green (fast market)

End this page with one sentence: "The [Month] market in [City] is a [Seller's / Balanced / Buyer's] market."

### Page 3 — What the Numbers Mean
Three short sections, each 2-3 paragraphs.

**Overall Market:**
What is the headline story this month? Is the market shifting? Staying consistent?
What's the most important trend in the data?

**For Buyers This Month:**
Speak directly to someone considering buying. What does this data mean for their
search, their offers, their timeline? Be specific and opinionated.
Example: "If you're buying under $600K in Calgary right now, you're competing with
roughly [X]% fewer buyers than this time last year. That's meaningful leverage — but
it won't last if rates come down."

**For Sellers This Month:**
Speak directly to someone considering listing. Pricing expectations, demand context,
timing advice. Be specific and opinionated.
Example: "Well-priced detached homes in [neighbourhood] are still selling in under
3 weeks. The operative word is 'well-priced' — overpriced listings are sitting."

### Page 4 — Property Type Breakdown
Three columns (or sections): Detached | Condo/Apartment | Townhouse

For each:
- Average/benchmark price
- YoY price change
- Sales volume
- Days on market
- One sentence of commentary

If only one property type is relevant to the agent's niche, expand that section and
condense or omit the others.

### Page 5 — Community Spotlight
Only include if the agent covers specific named communities in their knowledge file.

For each community:
- Average sale price (if available)
- Notable trend or data point
- 1-2 sentences of plain-English commentary

If community-level data isn't publicly available, note: "Community-level data not
publicly available — reach out for a custom analysis of [Community]."

### Page 6 — Rate & Affordability Context
Two parts:

**Current Rate Environment:**
- Current 5-year fixed rate (or most relevant product)
- Change since last month or last year
- What the Bank of Canada / Federal Reserve is signaling

**Real Affordability Numbers:**
Show 2-3 specific monthly payment examples using current rates and typical price
points in the agent's market. Format:

"$[Price] home | [X]% down | [X]% rate | ≈ $[Monthly] per month"

This makes the data tangible. Someone reading this should immediately understand
what their budget means in real dollar terms.

### Page 7 — Agent Commentary & CTA
The agent's voice. 2-3 sentences max. Their personal read on the market this month.
Pull tone from brand personality file.

Followed by:
- "What does this mean for your specific situation?"
- Booking link
- Phone / email
- Social handles
- "Know someone buying or selling in [City]? Share this with them."

## Report Quality Standards

- Every number has a source (listed in the Data Sources section of the .docx)
- Every number has context — not just the raw figure
- Plain English throughout — no jargon without explanation
- Agent's city/community named specifically on every page
- Total length: 7 pages as described above — not shorter, not longer
- Works as a standalone document without the video
