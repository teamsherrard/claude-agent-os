# Short-Form Guide — 3 Market Update Video Concepts

## Overview

Every market update generates 3 short-form video concepts. Each is a different format
so the agent has variety in their content feed that week. All three draw directly from
the market data researched for the update — no additional research needed.

The 3 formats:
1. Article-Based — reacts to a specific news article about the market
2. Green Screen Background — walks through data with stats displayed behind them
3. Talking Head Opinion — the agent's expert take based on what the data shows

---

## Concept 1 — Article-Based

### Purpose
The agent reacts to a real news article about their market — adding local expert
interpretation that turns a headline into useful advice for their audience.

### Article Selection
Find one article published within the last 7 days that is directly related to:
- The market stats covered in the update (board release, price data, inventory news)
- OR the rate/mortgage environment covered in the update
- OR a local development or policy story relevant to the agent's market

The article should have at least one specific stat or finding the agent can react to.
Verify the link is working before including it.

### Script Structure
```
HOOK (3-5 sec):
[Specific stat or headline from the article — city specific if possible]

CONTEXT (5-10 sec):
[What happened. Source name, number, date. No "according to this article" language.]

YOUR TAKE (25-40 sec):
[2-3 beats of local interpretation. What does this mean for buyers or sellers in
[city] right now? Agent's genuine opinion. Locally specific.]

CTA (5-10 sec):
[Single action — match to agent's primary CTA from Project file]
```

### Riff Notes
4-5 bullet points covering the same beats in shorthand for agents who prefer to ad lib.

### Caption
100-150 words. Opens with the hook adapted for reading. 2-3 lines of context.
CTA line. 8-10 hashtags including at minimum 2 local tags.

## Concept 2 — Green Screen Background

### Purpose
The agent stands in front of their screen or a displayed visual showing the key market
stats. They walk through the numbers as the local data authority. Numbers on screen
build instant credibility.

### Background Content Description
Describe exactly what the agent should display behind them. Be specific enough that
the agent can recreate it in Canva, on their phone, or by pulling up the report.

Format for the background description:
```
BACKGROUND TO DISPLAY:
Option A — Pull up Page 2 of your market update report (Market at a Glance page)
Option B — Create a simple graphic showing:
  - Headline: "[City] [Month] Market Update"
  - [Metric 1]: [Value] [YoY arrow]
  - [Metric 2]: [Value] [YoY arrow]
  - [Metric 3]: [Value] [YoY arrow]
  (Agent can build this in Canva in 5 minutes using their brand colors)
```

### Script Structure
Same 4-part structure as above but with `[ON SCREEN: text]` callouts integrated
throughout to direct the viewer's attention to specific numbers as the agent speaks.

```
HOOK (3-5 sec):
[ON SCREEN: City + Month + "Market Update"]
"[City]'s [Month] numbers just dropped — here's the only stat that matters right now."

CONTEXT (5-10 sec):
[Walk through 2-3 key stats visible on screen]
[ON SCREEN: highlight the key stat as you mention it]

YOUR TAKE (25-40 sec):
[What these numbers mean for the viewer right now]
[ON SCREEN: "What this means for buyers 👇" or "What this means for sellers 👇"]
[2-3 beats of local interpretation]

CTA (5-10 sec):
[ON SCREEN: CTA text — "Book a free call — link in bio"]
[Single action]
```

### Riff Notes
4-5 bullet points in shorthand.

### Caption
Same structure as Concept 1. Different hook angle from the article script.

## Concept 3 — Talking Head Opinion

### Purpose
Pure talking head — no background, no screen share. Just the agent and their camera.
This is the most personal format and builds the strongest connection.
The agent shares a genuine opinion or interpretation based on what the data shows.

### Angle Selection
Look at the data from this month's update and identify the single strongest opinion
angle. Pick the one that is most specific to this month's numbers — not a generic take.

**Angle options — pick the one that fits the data:**

**Contrarian:** The data shows something that contradicts what most people assume.
Example: "Everyone thinks it's a terrible time to buy. The [Month] data tells a
different story — here's what I'm actually seeing."

**Before/After:** A meaningful change from a year ago or 6 months ago.
Example: "[City] buyers 12 months ago vs today — here's what actually changed."

**Myth Buster:** A common belief in the market that the data disproves.
Example: "The most common thing I'm hearing from buyers is [X]. The numbers
say that's not quite right."

**Prediction:** Based on this month's data, what does the agent think happens next?
Example: "Based on what I'm seeing in the [Month] numbers, here's my honest
prediction for [City] real estate over the next 90 days."

**Neighbourhood Spotlight:** One specific community performing differently from
the overall market — interesting contrast angle.
Example: "[Community] is doing something completely different from the rest of
[City] right now — here's why."

**Buyer/Seller Advice:** Direct, specific advice for one type of viewer.
Example: "If I were a [first-time buyer / seller / investor] in [City] right now,
here's exactly what I would do."

### Script Structure
Same 4-part structure. Opening does NOT reference an article or a background.
Opens with the agent's opinion or observation directly.

```
HOOK (3-5 sec):
[Agent's bold opening statement — opinion or observation based on the data]

CONTEXT (5-10 sec):
[The data point or trend that supports the opening statement]

YOUR TAKE (25-40 sec):
[The full opinion — 2-3 beats. Specific. Locally relevant. Something the agent
genuinely believes and would tell a client.]

CTA (5-10 sec):
[Single action]
```

### Riff Notes
4-5 bullet points in shorthand.

### Caption
Same structure. Different hook angle from Concepts 1 and 2.
Caption for this one should feel more personal and opinion-driven — "My honest
take on [city] real estate this month..." energy.

---

## Cross-Concept Variety Rules

After writing all 3 concepts, verify:
- [ ] All 3 hooks use different formulas — no two open the same way
- [ ] All 3 are aimed at different viewer types (buyers, sellers, general local)
- [ ] Captions for all 3 have different opening lines
- [ ] No two concepts cover the exact same data point as their primary stat
- [ ] The 3 scripts together tell a complete story of the market — someone watching
      all 3 gets a fuller picture than watching just one
