# YouTube Script Guide — 15-Minute Market Update Video

## The Format

The agent sits or stands on camera with their screen share running — the market update report
is visible behind them or beside them as they talk. They advance through the report page by page
while the script tells them exactly what to say at each point.

This format works because:
- The data on screen builds credibility — viewers can see the real numbers
- The agent's commentary adds value — they explain what the numbers mean
- It's repeatable — same structure every month, agent just swaps the data
- Beginners can do it — they just read and click through

---

## Script Length Target

- **Target:** 15 minutes
- **Word count:** ~1,950 words at 130 words per minute
- **Tolerance:** 13-17 minutes is acceptable
- If the script runs short, expand the buyer and seller interpretation sections
- If it runs long, tighten the property type breakdown section

---

## Script Formatting Conventions

Use these consistently throughout every script:

- `[SCREEN: Show Page X — Page Title]` — tells agent to advance to the next page
- `[PAUSE]` — natural pause point, let data sink in
- `[CAMERA: look directly at camera]` — redirect attention from screen to viewer
- `[POINT: gesture to stat on screen]` — physical direction for on-screen gestures
- `(X min)` — estimated time marker at each section header

All directions in [BRACKETS] are for the agent only — they don't read them aloud.

---

## Section-by-Section Script Guide

### INTRO (60-90 seconds)

**Never open with:** "Hey guys, welcome back to my channel"
**Always open with:** The single most compelling stat from this month's data

The first line should make someone stop scrolling in the first 3 seconds.

**Intro formula:**
```
[CAMERA: look directly at camera]

[Compelling stat from this month — city specific, specific number.]

[One-sentence implication of that stat for buyers or sellers.]

My name is [Agent Name], I'm a real estate agent in [City], and every month
I break down exactly what's happening in our market so you have the real
numbers — not the headlines, not the national averages, the actual data
from [City].

[PRIMARY CTA (15-20 seconds) — the 2-CTA model puts the first CTA right after the
hook, not only at the end. One natural line with the agent's main offer + booking
link (from ~/realtor-brain/identity/voice.md / offer.md): e.g. "And if you want to
know what these numbers mean for YOUR move, I do free consultations — the link is
the first one in the description."]

[Brief statement of what this video covers]

Let's get into the [Month] numbers.

[SCREEN: Show Page 2 — Market at a Glance]
```

---

### SECTION 1 — MARKET AT A GLANCE (2-3 minutes)

Walk through each metric on Page 2 one by one. For each stat: read the number, give
it one sentence of plain-English context. Don't rush — let each number land.

**Per-metric pattern:**
- State the number
- Compare to last year
- One sentence on what it means

End this section with the market condition verdict:
"So the headline for [Month] in [City]: this is a [Seller's / Balanced / Buyer's] market,
and here's what that means depending on which side of the transaction you're on."

`[SCREEN: Show Page 3 — What the Numbers Mean]`

---

### SECTION 2 — WHAT THIS MEANS FOR BUYERS (2-3 minutes)

`[CAMERA: look at camera]`

Speak directly to buyers. The agent takes a clear position — not "it depends."
What would they tell a buyer who called them today?

Cover:
1. Competition level right now (is it more or less competitive than it was?)
2. Price trend (is this a good time to buy relative to recent history?)
3. Rate environment and what it means for purchasing power
4. One concrete strategic recommendation

**Tone:** Warm, direct, expert. Not salesy. The agent is helping them make a good
decision — not pushing them to buy.

`[SCREEN: Stay on Page 3 or advance to Page 6 — Rate & Affordability]`

---

### SECTION 3 — WHAT THIS MEANS FOR SELLERS (2-3 minutes)

`[CAMERA: look at camera]`

Speak directly to sellers. Again — clear position, not "it depends."

Cover:
1. Demand level (are buyers active? are homes moving?)
2. Pricing reality (what can sellers realistically expect?)
3. Days on market (how long should they expect to wait?)
4. One concrete strategic recommendation

**Tone:** Honest and direct. Good agents tell sellers the truth even when it's not
what they want to hear. This builds trust.

`[SCREEN: Show Page 4 — Property Type Breakdown]`

---

### SECTION 4 — PROPERTY TYPE BREAKDOWN (2 minutes)

Walk through detached, condo, and townhouse data.
Highlight the most interesting comparison or divergence between property types.
If one segment is performing significantly differently — call it out.

This section can be tightened or expanded based on what the data shows.
If all three types are moving in the same direction, keep it brief.
If there's a notable divergence, spend more time here.

`[SCREEN: Show Page 5 — Community Spotlight (if applicable)]`

---

### SECTION 5 — COMMUNITY SPOTLIGHT (1-2 minutes, if applicable)

Only include if the agent covers specific named communities.
Quick hits — 2-3 sentences per community.
What's moving, what's sitting, what's worth watching.

If no community data — skip this section and add the time to buyer/seller sections.

`[SCREEN: Show Page 6 — Rate & Affordability]`

---

### SECTION 6 — RATE & AFFORDABILITY (1-2 minutes)

Walk through the real affordability numbers.
Read the monthly payment examples from the report.

"At today's rates, a buyer putting [X]% down on a [City] home in the [price range]
range is looking at roughly $[X] per month. Six months ago that same home cost
$[X]/month. That's [more/less] — and that [matters / is why buyers are / are not]..."

Connect rates to buyer behavior — are rates bringing people off the sidelines or
keeping them away? What's the agent actually seeing?

`[SCREEN: Show Page 7 — Agent Commentary]`

---

### OUTRO (60-90 seconds)

`[CAMERA: look directly at camera]`

Three-part outro:

**1. Summary (3 key takeaways):**
"So to wrap up [Month] in [City] — three things to remember:
One: [Most important takeaway]
Two: [Second takeaway]
Three: [Third takeaway]"

**2. Secondary CTA:**
"If you're thinking about buying or selling in [City] and want to know what any of
this means for your specific situation — I do free consultations. Link is in the
description below."

**3. Subscribe ask:**
"If you want this market update every single month — hit subscribe. I do this every
month so you always have the real numbers for [City]. No fluff, no national averages,
just what's actually happening here."

`[CAMERA: gesture to end screen]`
"Check out last month's update right here."

## Script Quality Rules

- First word of the video is the stat — not the agent's name or a greeting
- "You" or "your" appears throughout both buyer and seller sections
- City is named by name at least once per section — never "locally" or "in our market"
- Agent takes a clear position in both buyer and seller sections — no "it depends" hedging
- All numbers in the script match the numbers in the report exactly
- Every section cue `[SCREEN: ...]` matches a real page in the report
- Two CTAs (the 2-CTA model): a primary CTA right after the hook/intro AND a secondary CTA in the outro
- Outro includes: summary, secondary CTA, subscribe ask, end card direction
- Total word count is between 1,700 and 2,100 words
