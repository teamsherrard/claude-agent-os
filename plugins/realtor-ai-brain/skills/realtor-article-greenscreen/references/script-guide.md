# Script Guide — Realtor Green Screen Article Talking Scripts

## The Core Concept

The agent stands in front of their green screen. Behind them plays a background showing a real
news headline and stat. The agent reacts to it — like a local expert giving their take on the news.

The background is the evidence. The agent is the expert.

This format works because it combines credibility (real news source) with trust (local agent's
interpretation). Viewers stop scrolling because the headline grabs them. They keep watching
because the agent sounds like they actually know what they're talking about.

---

## What Makes a Great Green Screen Article Script

### 1. The agent sounds like they already know this — not like they just read it
BAD: "I came across this article today that says home prices in Calgary dropped by 3%..."
GOOD: "Calgary prices just dropped 3% month over month. Here's what that actually means if you're thinking about buying right now."

The background shows the article. The agent doesn't need to cite it. They just react.

### 2. The hook uses the stat or headline directly
The first line should reference the news immediately — don't build up to it.
BAD: "Hey guys, I wanted to talk about something important happening in the market today..."
GOOD: "Inventory in Calgary just hit its lowest point in 3 years. That's not a headline — that's your window."

### 3. The take is local and specific
National news means nothing without local context. Every script must answer:
"What does this mean for someone buying or selling in [city] RIGHT NOW?"

BAD: "This is happening across the country and it's something all buyers should be aware of."
GOOD: "In [city], what this means is that if you're looking in the $600K-$800K range, you're competing with fewer buyers than you were six months ago. That's leverage. Use it."

### 4. The agent has an opinion
Agents who just repeat the news are boring. Agents who add their take are memorable.
The script should include one moment where the agent says something opinionated:
- "Honestly? This is the best news sellers have had all year."
- "I know that sounds scary, but here's why I'm actually telling my clients to keep going."
- "Most agents will tell you to wait. I disagree."

### 5. The CTA is simple and low-pressure
One action. Not three.
Beginners should use: "Follow for [city] market updates every week" or "Link in bio to book a free call."

## Script Length by Format

| Video Length | Word Count | Best For |
|---|---|---|
| 15 seconds | ~40 words | Single stat, one-line take, bold opinion |
| 30 seconds | ~75 words | Stat + local context + CTA |
| 45 seconds | ~110 words | Stat + 2-point take + CTA |
| 60 seconds | ~150 words | Full structure — hook, context, take, CTA |

Default to 45 seconds for article videos. Long enough to add value, short enough to hold attention.

---

## Script Template (Fill in for Each Article)

```
HOOK (5 sec):
[City] just [key stat or development from article]. [One-line implication or reaction.]

CONTEXT (10 sec):
[What happened, briefly. No "according to" language. 1-2 sentences max.
Mention the specific number, city, and time frame.]

YOUR TAKE (30 sec):
[What this means for buyers OR sellers OR investors in [city] right now.
Include one specific, opinionated statement the agent stands behind.
Reference a local detail — neighbourhood, price range, current conditions.
Speak directly to one type of viewer, not everyone at once.]

CTA (10 sec):
[One action. Match to agent's primary CTA from their brand/profile file.]
```

## On-Screen Text Callout Rules

Include 2-3 `[ON SCREEN: ___]` suggestions inline with the script. These are text overlays the
agent (or their editor) adds in CapCut, Instagram Reels editor, or TikTok.

Rules:
- Max 6 words per callout
- Use for: key stats, city name, key phrases, CTA keyword
- Place them where the agent says the most important line
- Don't use them for every line — only the 2-3 moments that most need visual reinforcement

Examples:
`[ON SCREEN: Calgary inventory: lowest since 2022]`
`[ON SCREEN: What this means for buyers 👇]`
`[ON SCREEN: Book a free call — link in bio]`

## Tone Calibration by Audience

Pull from the agent's brand personality file. Default guidance if not found:

| Audience | Tone | What They Need to Hear |
|---|---|---|
| First-time buyers | Reassuring, educational | "You're not too late. Here's your move." |
| Move-up buyers | Confident, strategic | "This creates an opportunity. Here's how to use it." |
| Sellers | Direct, market-savvy | "This is what the data means for your asking price." |
| Investors | Data-driven, analytical | "Here's what this shift means for your yield." |
| General local | Warm, community-expert | "This is what's happening in our market right now." |

---

## Phrases to Never Use in Scripts

- "According to this article..."
- "I read that..."
- "A recent news story says..."
- "In today's ever-changing market..."
- "As your local real estate professional..."
- "Your dream home awaits..."
- "Now more than ever..."
- "Reach out to me for all your real estate needs"
- "I'm here to help you buy or sell"
- "The market is always a great time to invest"

These kill credibility instantly. Every agent sounds like this. Don't.
