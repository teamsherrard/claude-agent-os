## Best Search Patterns

### For market stats
`site:crea.ca OR site:creb.com OR site:nar.realtor "[city]" [month] [year]`
`"[city] real estate" statistics [month] [year]`
`"[city]" housing market report [month] [year]`

### For rate news
`Bank of Canada rate decision [month] [year]`
`mortgage rate [country] [month] [year]`
`"fixed rate" OR "variable rate" forecast [year]`

### For local news
`"[city]" new development real estate [year]`
`"[city]" rezoning OR "new housing" [year]`
`"[city]" first time home buyer [month] [year]`

### For national context
`Canada housing market [month] [year]`
`US housing market outlook [month] [year]`
`real estate forecast [year]`

## Article Freshness Rules

| Age | Status | Action |
|-----|--------|--------|
| 0-3 days | Ideal | Always use if relevant |
| 4-7 days | Acceptable | Use if no fresher alternatives |
| 8-14 days | Borderline | Only use for evergreen topics (rates, policy) |
| 15+ days | Do not use | Skip entirely |

## Reading the Article Before Using It

Before selecting an article, confirm:
1. What is the single most compelling data point or headline?
2. Can a real estate agent in [city] add meaningful commentary to this?
3. Would a buyer or seller in [city] care about this this week?
4. Is the data point specific enough to display on a background? (e.g., "sales up 12%" is displayable. "market conditions remain mixed" is not.)

If you can't answer yes to all four — find a different article.
