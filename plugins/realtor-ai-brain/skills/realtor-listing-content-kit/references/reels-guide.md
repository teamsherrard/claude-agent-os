# Reels Guide — 3 Short-Form Listing Scripts

## Core Rule for All 3 Scripts

Every Reel must work as completely standalone content. A viewer who has never
seen the YouTube walkthrough should get full value from each script on its own.

No script should say "watch my full video for the complete tour" as its hook.
No script should depend on the viewer having watched anything else first.

---

## The 3 Angles — Why Each One Exists

**Reel 1 — Just Listed Announcement**
Pure awareness. Posted the moment the listing goes live. Designed for maximum reach.
The goal is to stop the scroll and get as many relevant eyeballs on this listing as possible.
Energy: fast, factual, excited without being salesy.

**Reel 2 — Top 3 Features**
Deeper consideration. For buyers who stopped scrolling on the announcement and want more.
Showcases the 3 most compelling selling points for the specific target buyer.
Energy: confident, specific, almost conversational — like explaining to a friend why this home is worth seeing.

**Reel 3 — Neighbourhood Angle**
Lifestyle and location. For buyers who are evaluating the area as much as the home.
Sells the zip code, not just the property.
Energy: local expert, genuinely enthusiastic about where this home is located.

---

## Reel 1 — Just Listed Announcement

### Hook options by target buyer type:

*First-time buyers:*
"Just listed in [Neighbourhood] under $[X] — and this one actually has a [key feature]."

*Families:*
"New listing in [Neighbourhood] — [X] beds, [catchment school name] school district, $[X]."

*Upsizers:*
"Just listed in [Neighbourhood] — [X] sq ft, [key feature], asking $[X]."

*Investors:*
"New listing in [Neighbourhood] — [X] beds, separate entrance, $[X]. [One-line investment angle.]"

*Downsizers:*
"Just listed in [Neighbourhood] — lock and leave [property type], [key feature], $[X]."

### Body talking points (15-20 seconds):
Pull the 4 most important facts from the listing:
- Bedroom and bathroom count
- Key standout feature (the one thing that makes this listing memorable)
- Price and how it compares to the neighbourhood
- One neighbourhood fact (school, amenity, or location perk)

### What NOT to do in Reel 1:
- Don't list every feature — this is awareness, not a full pitch
- Don't oversell — let the facts do the work
- Don't open with the address — open with what's compelling about it

## Reel 2 — Top 3 Features

### Feature Selection Rule

Pick the 3 features most relevant to the target buyer — not the 3 most impressive
features to any buyer. They're not always the same thing.

Examples:
- Schools angle → catchment school by name, backyard size, bedroom count
- For first-time buyers → price vs neighbourhood avg, updated kitchen, low maintenance
- For investors → suite potential, rental income estimate, location to transit
- For downsizers → main floor primary bedroom, lock-and-leave, low maintenance yard

### Hook formulas:

"Three things that make [Address / 'this [Neighbourhood] home'] worth a second look."
"Here are the three features my buyers keep asking about in this listing."
"If you're looking in [Neighbourhood] in this price range — these three things stand out."

### Feature presentation format:

Each feature follows the same pattern:
1. Name it boldly — "Number one: [Feature name]"
2. Describe it specifically — not "great kitchen" but "renovated in 2023, quartz counters,
   10-foot island, KitchenAid appliances"
3. Buyer-relevant so-what — "which means [what this means for this specific buyer]"

[ON SCREEN: Feature name as text overlay while agent is describing it]

### Filming note:
Film each feature at the actual location in the home.
Don't stand in one spot for the whole video — move to each feature as you describe it.
The movement makes the video more dynamic and more credible.

## Reel 3 — Neighbourhood Angle

### The Core Idea

This script is about WHERE the home is, not what's inside it.
It's for the buyer who's already thinking about the neighbourhood and needs to know
if this specific home puts them in the right position within it.

Film outside — on the street in front of the home, at a nearby café or park, or
while walking to a nearby amenity. The neighbourhood should be visually present.

### Hook formulas:

"You're not just buying [Address]. You're buying into [Neighbourhood]. Here's what that means."
"[Neighbourhood] in [City] is [specific compelling quality]. Here's a listing that puts you right in the middle of it."
"If [Neighbourhood] is on your shortlist — here's a listing that's worth your time."

### Neighbourhood talking points (from research):
Pick 3 specific, named facts that matter most to the target buyer — angle on facts about the place,
never on who "belongs" there (fair housing):

Schools angle: school name + rating, park or playground name, walking distance to a named green space
Commute angle: commute time to downtown, café or restaurant name, walkability score
For first-time buyers (by budget): price vs neighbourhood average, transit access, what the neighbourhood is becoming
For downsizers: quiet street context, proximity to amenities, maintenance-free lifestyle angle

### Listing bridge (5-10 seconds):
After covering the neighbourhood, connect back to the specific property.
"And [Address] puts you [X minutes / steps / blocks] from all of that — listed at $[X]."

### What NOT to do in Reel 3:
- Don't lead with the property — the neighbourhood is the star of this video
- Don't use generic neighbourhood language ("great community feel")
- Always name specific places — never "nearby schools" or "local restaurants"

## Caption Rules — All 3 Reels

**Structure:**
- Line 1: Hook adapted for reading (different from the spoken hook but same angle)
- Lines 2-3: Key details — property specifics or neighbourhood highlights depending on the reel
- Line 4: CTA
- Line 5+: Hashtags

**Hashtag formula (8-10 total):**
- 2 local: #[City]RealEstate + #[Neighbourhood] or #[City]Homes
- 2 property type: #[PropertyType]ForSale + #[City][PropertyType]
- 2 audience: #[TargetBuyer] + #HomeSearch or #HomeBuying[City]
- 2-3 broad: #RealEstate + #[City]Realtor + #JustListed

**Caption tone rules:**
- No "stunning" / "gorgeous" / "dream home" in captions — same as scripts
- Specific over vague — always
- Reel 3 caption should feel different from Reels 1 and 2 — neighbourhood-first energy
- All 3 captions must have different opening lines

---

## Cross-Reel Variety Checklist

- [ ] All 3 hooks use different formulas
- [ ] All 3 are filmed at different locations (inside for Reel 1+2, outside for Reel 3)
- [ ] No two Reels lead with the same feature or stat
- [ ] Captions for all 3 have completely different first lines
- [ ] Reel 3 does not lead with the property — neighbourhood first
