# YouTube Script Guide — Room-by-Room Listing Walkthrough

## The Format

The agent films themselves walking through the property — room by room — while
talking to camera. They move through the home the way a buyer would on a showing.
The script gives them the talking points at each stop so they never go blank.

This is different from a sit-down market update. The agent is moving. The camera
is moving. The energy should feel like a private showing — not a TV commercial.

---

## The Tone

**Sounds like:** An agent giving a private showing to one specific buyer.
Informative, honest, specific. Pointing things out naturally. Comfortable in the space.

**Never sounds like:**
- A property listing description ("boasting stunning finishes throughout")
- A TV real estate show ("this incredible space really speaks to you")
- A sales pitch ("you're going to absolutely love this")
- Generic real estate language ("nestled in a sought-after neighbourhood")

**The test:** Read it out loud. Does it sound like something you'd actually say
while walking through a home with a buyer? If not, rewrite it.

---

## Words to Never Use in Listing Scripts

These are the most overused words in real estate content. Every agent uses them.
Using them makes the video forgettable.

❌ Stunning / stunning views
❌ Gorgeous / gorgeous finishes
❌ Dream home / dream kitchen
❌ Nestled / tucked away
❌ Cozy (unless genuinely small — be honest)
❌ Boasting / featuring
❌ Sought-after
❌ Rare find
❌ Must see
❌ Turnkey (unless you explain specifically what's been done)
❌ Immaculate / pristine
❌ Spacious (always say the actual square footage instead)

Replace every one of these with something specific.
Instead of "stunning kitchen" → "The kitchen was fully renovated in 2023 —
new quartz counters, KitchenAid appliances, and a 10-foot island."

---

## Room-by-Room Script Rules

### Every room needs three things:
1. **What to point the camera at** — a specific visual direction in [brackets]
2. **What to say** — talking points in natural spoken language
3. **A transition line** — how to move naturally to the next space

### Time guidelines per room:
| Room | Target time |
|------|-------------|
| Hook | 30-45 sec |
| Exterior | 60-90 sec |
| Entryway | 30-60 sec |
| Main living areas | 90-120 sec each |
| Kitchen | 60-90 sec |
| Primary bedroom | 60-90 sec |
| Additional bedrooms | 30-60 sec each |
| Bathrooms | 30-45 sec each |
| Basement/bonus | 60-90 sec |
| Outdoor space | 60-90 sec |
| Neighbourhood close | 60-90 sec |
| Closing line | 30-45 sec |

Total target: 8-15 minutes depending on property size.

---

## Transition Lines — How to Write Them

Transitions happen while the agent physically moves between rooms.
They should feel seamless — not "okay now let's go to the kitchen."

**Good transition formulas:**

*Contrast transition:*
"So that's the [current room] — and what I like about this floor plan is how
naturally it flows into the [next room]. Come with me."

*Feature tease:*
"Now the kitchen — this is actually one of my favourite parts of this home.
[move camera toward kitchen]"

*Buyer bridge:*
"If you're a [target buyer type], this is the part of the home I really want
you to see. [move to next room]"

*Simple and natural:*
"Let me show you the [next room]. [start walking]"

---

## Kitchen — Always Spend Time Here

The kitchen is the room buyers make decisions in. Give it more time than any
other room except the primary bedroom.

Always cover:
- Counter space (linear feet if notable)
- Storage — number of cabinets, pantry
- Island or breakfast bar (dimensions if notable)
- Appliances — brand and age if recently updated
- Any renovation or upgrade with approximate year
- Natural light — windows, skylights
- Connection to dining room or outdoor space

---

## Primary Bedroom — Second Most Important Room

Always cover:
- Room size with practical context ("fits a king with nightstands on both sides")
- Natural light and windows
- Walk-in closet vs standard (dimensions if notable)
- Ensuite details — fixtures, double vanity, soaker tub, shower, heated floors

---

## Honest Observations Build More Trust Than Perfect Pitches

If a room is small, say: "This second bedroom is more compact — works well as
a nursery, a home office, or a guest room for someone who doesn't need a massive space."

If parking is limited: "Street parking only here — worth mentioning if you have
multiple cars."

If the basement is unfinished: "The basement is unfinished right now — clean slate
if you want to add a rec room or extra bedroom down the line. The rough-in for a
bathroom is already there."

Honest observations make the agent credible. Buyers will see the home in person.
Better to set expectations than have them feel misled.

---

## The Neighbourhood Close

This is the section that turns a property video into a community video.
Film outside — on the street, in the front yard, or at a nearby amenity.

Always include:
- Catchment schools by name (from research)
- 1-2 named nearby spots — café, park, restaurant, transit
- Commute context — "[X] minutes to downtown by [car/transit]"
- One observation about the street or community vibe

This section directly addresses the buyer who's evaluating "do I want to live HERE"
not just "do I want this house."
