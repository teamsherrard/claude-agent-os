# Target Avatar — Real Estate Agent Content Creator

## Who This Content Speaks To

A residential real estate agent in the US or Canada who wants to grow their local brand through
YouTube and short-form content. They are serious about their business but have never had a real
system for content. They want to look professional, sound knowledgeable, and attract clients
from their local market — without spending hours figuring out what to post.

## Two Common Profiles

**The Self-Starter (2-8 years in the business):** Has hustle and market knowledge but no
content system. Knows they should be on YouTube but can't figure out what to say. Posts
inconsistently. Wants to be seen as the go-to agent in their market.

**The Established Agent (8+ years):** Has a strong reputation locally but almost no online
presence. Gets most business from referrals and doesn't want to lose ground as the market
shifts toward digital. Skeptical of social media but knows they need to adapt.

## How They Think About Content

- They have real knowledge about their market — they just can't translate it into content
- They're afraid of saying the wrong thing or looking amateur on camera
- They don't know what topics will actually attract buyers or sellers
- They assume they need to be an "influencer" — they don't realize local authority is the goal
- They want content that builds trust and brings in consultations, not views for the sake of views

## What They Want From This Skill

- A complete package they can hand to a VA or act on themselves
- Word-for-word scripts they can just read — no figuring out what to say
- Content that sounds like THEM (local, knowledgeable, approachable) not like a generic real estate ad
- Multiple pieces of content from one piece of effort
- To feel like a professional content creator without needing to become one

## Their Market Context

Every agent's market is different. Always reference the specific city/market the agent provides.
Content that says "in [City]" and references local conditions performs better than generic advice.
The local angle is the entire differentiation strategy.

## Tone Rules for All Content

**Always write for:** One potential buyer or seller in the agent's specific city who is doing
research online. They don't know the agent yet. They're evaluating trust.

**Tone must be:** Warm, credible, local, clear. Like a knowledgeable friend who happens to
know the market inside out.

**Never sound like:** A corporate real estate ad, a generic motivational post, or a
salesperson. No "Your dream home awaits!" No "In today's competitive market..."

**Always make the viewer/reader feel:** Informed, in good hands, like this agent actually
knows what they're talking about — and cares about helping them.

## The Internal Filter

Before generating any deliverable, check:
- Does this sound like it comes from a real local expert?
- Would someone in [City] find this useful and trustworthy?
- Does this make the agent look credible — not desperate for business?
- Would a buyer or seller feel informed after reading/watching this?

If not — rewrite.

## Final Anchor

The agent's content exists to say: "I know this market. I know what you're going through.
When you're ready, I'm the person you call."
