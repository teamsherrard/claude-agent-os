# Voice Guide — Real Estate Agent Content

## The Core Voice Principle

Real estate agent content must sound like it comes from a knowledgeable local person —
not a marketing department, not a generic AI, and not a motivational speaker.

The agent is a trusted local expert. Their voice is warm, credible, direct, and market-specific.
Every word should reinforce one thing: this person actually knows what they're talking about.

---

## Voice Characteristics

### Sentence Structure
- Clear and conversational. Short sentences for emphasis. No run-ons.
- Direct second-person address: "If you're thinking about buying in [City] this year..."
- Use the viewer's situation to frame the content: "Most first-time buyers in [City] don't realize..."
- Contrast pairs for emphasis: "In 2023 this market was [X]. In 2026 it looks completely different."

### Tone Spectrum by Content Type

**Educational / Authority** (YouTube scripts, SEO descriptions, LinkedIn):
- Expert delivery, clear structure, specific data
- "Here's exactly what the numbers show" energy
- Local stats over national generalizations
- Confident but not arrogant

**Warm / Local** (Reels, emails, Instagram captions, Facebook):
- Conversational, like texting a neighbour
- Speak from experience, not from a script
- Relatable observations about the local market
- Zero corporate language

---

## What This Voice NEVER Sounds Like

- Generic real estate marketing: "Your dream home awaits!" / "Making your real estate dreams a reality"
- Corporate jargon: "Leverage our expertise" / "Best-in-class service" / "Optimize your buying experience"
- Hollow AI output: "In today's ever-changing real estate landscape..." / "As a seasoned real estate professional..."
- Desperate or pushy: "Call me NOW before it's too late!" / "Don't miss this opportunity!"
- Vague platitudes: "The market is always a great time to buy" / "It's all about location, location, location"

---

## Real Estate Voice Patterns That Work

### Local Specificity (Always Name the Market)
WEAK: "The housing market has seen some interesting shifts lately."
STRONG: "Calgary's detached home market just hit [X] days on market — down from [Y] last spring."

### Stat-Anchored Claims
WEAK: "A lot of buyers are competing for homes right now."
STRONG: "We had 3 offers on this listing in the first 48 hours. That's what [City] looks like right now."

### Viewer-Situation Framing
WEAK: "Buying a home is a big decision."
STRONG: "If you're thinking about buying your first home in [City] this year, the process has changed a lot from what you might have heard."

### Credibility Without Bragging
WEAK: "I'm the best agent in the city."
STRONG: "I've helped [X] families buy in [Neighbourhood] over the last 3 years, and here's what they all asked me first."

## Voice Calibration by Deliverable

| Deliverable | Tone | Length | Key Quality |
|---|---|---|---|
| Promotional email | Warm, direct | 80-150 words | Makes them click the video |
| YouTube description | SEO-rich, readable | 150-300 words | Local keywords, natural flow |
| Instagram caption | Story hook, local | 150-250 words | Hooks before "more" truncation |
| TikTok caption | Short, native | 50-100 words | Trending energy, local angle |
| LinkedIn post | Authority, professional | 250-400 words | Insight-led, not listing-led |
| Facebook post | Conversational | 100-200 words | Community tone |
| Reel scripts | Word-for-word spoken | 30-60 seconds | Natural, not read-y |
| Thumbnail brief | Technical / directive | N/A | Clear specs for designer |
| ManyChat DM | Warm, helpful, brief | 2-4 messages | Delivers value, not pitch |

---

## Sign-Off Style

Emails and DMs close warmly and personally:
- "[Agent first name]" on its own line — no corporate sign-offs
- Optional: "Talk soon, [Name]" or "Cheers, [Name]" — never "Best regards" or "Sincerely"

CTAs close conversationally:
- "Drop your questions in the comments — I read them all."
- "Book a free call and let's figure out your next move."
- "DM me [KEYWORD] and I'll send it over."
