# Hook Formulas Reference

## Hook Philosophy
The hook is the most important line of every deliverable. It determines whether someone watches,
reads, or scrolls past. Write the hook FIRST, then build everything else to deliver on its promise.

## 10 Core Hook Formulas

Every hook speaks to ONE potential buyer or seller in the agent's city (per `realtor-avatar.md`) —
never to other agents.

| # | Formula | When to Use | Example |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Problem-Agitation** | When addressing a common frustration | "Most [City] buyers spend months scrolling listings — and still miss the homes that actually fit their budget." |
| 2 | **Contrarian** | When challenging conventional wisdom | "Most buyers think they need 20% down. In [City] right now, that's just not true." |
| 3 | **Outcome Promise** | When the value stack is strong | "By the end of this video, you'll know exactly what it costs to own a home in [Neighbourhood] — down to the monthly payment." |
| 4 | **Story Tease** | When a personal story adds credibility | "Last month I showed a first-time buyer eleven homes in [City]. The one they bought? Nobody else had even asked about it." |
| 5 | **Authority** | When experience backs the claim | "I've helped [X] families buy in [Neighbourhood] over the last three years — here's what they all wish they'd known sooner." |
| 6 | **Stat-Led** | When a surprising number creates urgency | "Homes in [City] are sitting [X] days longer than last year. If you're selling, that changes everything." |
| 7 | **Fear / Expose** | When urgency is the goal (use carefully) | "The [City] listing that looks like a deal usually isn't. Here's what the photos aren't showing you." |
| 8 | **The Split** | When contrasting two groups | "The [City] market is splitting in two right now. Not by price. Not by neighbourhood. By one thing." |
| 9 | **Myth Buster** | When busting a common misconception | "You don't need a perfect credit score to buy in [City]. Most of my buyers this year didn't have one." |
| 10 | **Direct Address** | When calling out a specific feeling | "If you're trying to buy in [City] and feeling priced out — it's not you. It's how you're searching." |

## Hook Rules

### For Video Scripts:
- **Max 10 words** for on-screen text overlays
- Full spoken hook can be 2-4 sentences
- Must answer "What is this about?" and "Is this for me?" in under 3 seconds
- Start with the hook immediately — never ease into it

### For Email Subject Lines:
- Under 50 characters ideal
- Create a curiosity gap or promise a specific outcome
- Personalization ([Name]) boosts open rates when used sparingly

### For Instagram Captions:
- First sentence IS the hook — it appears before the "more" truncation
- Must be compelling enough to make them tap "more"
- Match the energy of the spoken video hook but in caption format

### For LinkedIn Posts:
- First 2-3 lines appear before the "see more" fold
- Use line breaks to create visual breathing room
- The hook should work as a standalone statement

## First-Frame On-Screen Text Formulas (for video)

| Formula | Example |
|---|---|
| Contrarian | "Stop [Common Practice]. Do This Instead." |
| Mistake | "The #1 Mistake [Audience] Make with [Topic]." |
| Number | "[Number] Ways to [Achieve Desired Outcome]." |
| Time-Based | "How to [Achieve Result] in [Short Timeframe]." |
| Question | "Are You [Making This Common Mistake]?" |
| Comparison | "[Old Way] vs [New Way]" |

## Hook Adaptation by Deliverable

The same core hook concept should be adapted for each platform — not copy-pasted:

| Deliverable | Hook Style | Length |
|---|---|---|
| Reel hook line | Spoken, stops the scroll | 1 sentence, lands in the first 3 seconds |
| TikTok/Facebook caption opener | Short punchy statement | 1 sentence, under 15 words |
| Email subject line | Curiosity or promise | Under 50 characters |
| Instagram caption opener | Story-driven version of the hook | 1-2 sentences |
| LinkedIn post opener | Thought-leadership version | 2-3 short lines |
| YouTube description opener | SEO-rich keyword version | 1-2 sentences with keywords |

The hook should feel consistent across all platforms but native to each format.
