# Hook Formulas Reference

## Hook Philosophy
The hook is the most important line of every deliverable. It determines whether someone watches,
reads, or scrolls past. Write the hook FIRST, then build everything else to deliver on its promise.

## 10 Core Hook Formulas

| # | Formula | When to Use | Example |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Problem-Agitation** | When addressing a common frustration | "Most agents spend hours trying to figure out TikTok. And by the time they publish something, they're so burned out they don't post again for two weeks." |
| 2 | **Contrarian** | When challenging conventional wisdom | "If you're using ChatGPT to create your real estate content, you're leaving quality on the table." |
| 3 | **Outcome Promise** | When the value stack is strong | "By the end of this video, you will have a complete, repeatable system for creating real estate TikToks using AI." |
| 4 | **Story Tease** | When a personal story adds credibility | "A few months ago, I tested every major AI tool to find the fastest way to create real estate TikToks." |
| 5 | **Authority** | When experience backs the claim | "I've helped hundreds of real estate agents build content systems that generate inbound leads on autopilot." |
| 6 | **Stat-Led** | When a surprising number creates urgency | "82% of buyers now use AI for housing research. Most agents aren't showing up." |
| 7 | **Fear / Expose** | When urgency is the goal (use carefully) | "AI is not going to make you successful. What it will do is expose every single weakness in your business." |
| 8 | **The Split** | When contrasting two groups | "The real estate industry is splitting in two right now. Not by market. Not by experience. By one thing." |
| 9 | **Myth Buster** | When busting a common misconception | "You don't need 10,000 followers to get clients. Most agents getting leads have small audiences." |
| 10 | **Direct Address** | When calling out a specific feeling | "If you're a real estate agent feeling overwhelmed in 2026, it's not the market. It's not the competition." |

## Hook Rules

### For Video Scripts / Carousel Hooks:
- **Max 10 words** for on-screen text overlays
- Full spoken hook can be 2-4 sentences
- Must answer "What is this about?" and "Is this for me?" in under 3 seconds
- Start with the hook immediately — never ease into it

### For Email Subject Lines:
- Under 50 characters ideal
- Create a curiosity gap or promise a specific outcome
- Personalization ([Name]) boosts open rates when used sparingly

### For Instagram Captions:
- First sentence IS the hook — it appears before the "more" truncation
- Must be compelling enough to make them tap "more"
- Match the energy of the carousel hook but in caption format

### For LinkedIn Posts:
- First 2-3 lines appear before the "see more" fold
- Use line breaks to create visual breathing room
- The hook should work as a standalone statement

## First-Frame On-Screen Text Formulas (for carousels and video)

| Formula | Example |
|---|---|
| Contrarian | "Stop [Common Practice]. Do This Instead." |
| Mistake | "The #1 Mistake [Audience] Make with [Topic]." |
| Number | "[Number] Ways to [Achieve Desired Outcome]." |
| Time-Based | "How to [Achieve Result] in [Short Timeframe]." |
| Question | "Are You [Making This Common Mistake]?" |
| Comparison | "[Old Way] vs [New Way]" |

## Hook Adaptation by Deliverable

The same core hook concept should be adapted for each platform — not copy-pasted:

| Deliverable | Hook Style | Length |
|---|---|---|
| Thumbnail text | Ultra-short (2-3 words) | "FREE LEADS" |
| Carousel Slide 1 | Short punchy statement | 1 sentence, under 15 words |
| Email subject line | Curiosity or promise | Under 50 characters |
| Instagram caption opener | Story-driven version of the hook | 1-2 sentences |
| LinkedIn post opener | Thought-leadership version | 2-3 short lines |
| YouTube description opener | SEO-rich keyword version | 1-2 sentences with keywords |

The hook should feel consistent across all platforms but native to each format.
