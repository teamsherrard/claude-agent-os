# Guidebook Template — Public-Facing "Why Work With Me" Document

## Purpose

This document goes to prospects. It is a lead magnet, a consultation leave-behind,
an email attachment, and a credibility builder. A buyer or seller should read it and
feel confident they've found the right agent before they've even had a phone call.

It must look like something worth keeping. Clean layout. Clear sections. Specific
enough to be credible. Written in the agent's voice.

---

## Tone and Writing Rules

- Written in first person — this is the agent speaking directly to a prospect
- Pull tone from the brand persona file — match the agent's voice exactly
- Specific over vague — every offer item has a concrete detail
- Benefit-driven — not "I do X" but "here's what X means for you"
- No real estate jargon without explanation
- No hollow phrases ("passionate about real estate", "your trusted advisor")
- Speak to the agent's specific target avatar(s) from the brand persona file —
  use their language, their fears, their goals

---

## Document Structure

### COVER PAGE

```
[AGENT NAME]
Your [City] Real Estate Guide

Why Work With [First Name]
— and what you get when you do

[Agent headshot placeholder]
[City] | [Year]
[Booking link]
```

---

### PAGE 1 — A NOTE FROM [AGENT FIRST NAME]

Short personal introduction. 3-4 paragraphs. Written in the agent's voice.

Paragraph 1: Who they are and why they do this work.
Pull from Q1 and the brand persona file. Make it human, not a bio.

Paragraph 2: Who they serve and what they understand about their clients' situation.
Reference the target avatar's specific context — first-time buyers navigating a
competitive market, sellers trying to maximize value in a shifting market, families
relocating to [city] without knowing the neighbourhoods yet. Speak to their reality.

Paragraph 3: What the agent commits to.
One genuine, specific commitment. Not "I'll work hard for you" —
something real. "I'll tell you the truth about price even when it's not what you
want to hear, because getting it right matters more than getting the listing."

Paragraph 4: Closing line and invitation.
"Here's exactly what working with me looks like."

---

### PAGE 2 — WHAT YOU GET WHEN YOU WORK WITH [FIRST NAME]

The full offer broken down clearly. This is the core of the document.

**Format:** A titled list of offer items. Each item has:
- A short bold title (3-5 words)
- 2-3 sentences explaining what it is and what it means for the client

Organize into sections if the agent serves both buyers and sellers:

**FOR BUYERS**
[Offer item 1 — bold title]
[2-3 sentence description — specific, benefit-driven]

[Offer item 2]
[Description]

[Continue for all buyer offer items]

**FOR SELLERS**
[Offer item 1]
[Description]

[Continue for all seller offer items]

**FOR EVERYONE**
[Items that apply to all clients — communication style, network access,
process support, guarantees, etc.]

*Writing rule for every offer item:*
BAD: "Professional photography"
GOOD: "Professional Photography & Video — Every listing includes a professional
photographer and video walkthrough at no additional cost. Homes with professional
photos sell 32% faster — and yours won't be the exception."

BAD: "Access to my vendor network"
GOOD: "Trusted Vendor Network — When you need a mortgage broker, lawyer,
inspector, stager, or contractor, I have vetted professionals I trust and have
worked with repeatedly. You don't have to find them — I introduce you."

---

### PAGE 3 — MY PROCESS

Walk through what working with this agent actually looks like from start to finish.
Organized as a simple numbered or visual step-by-step.

For buyers: from first conversation to keys in hand.
For sellers: from listing consultation to sold sign to closing.

If the agent serves both, do a brief version for each.

**Format:** Numbered steps. Each step has a title and 1-2 sentences.

Example:
```
Step 1 — The Consultation (Week 1)
We start with a free 30-minute call where I learn exactly what you're looking for
and walk you through what the [city] market looks like right now for your situation.

Step 2 — [Next step]
[Description]
```

Keep this to 5-7 steps maximum per track. Clean and readable.

---

### PAGE 4 — RESULTS THAT SPEAK FOR THEMSELVES

The proof page. Specific numbers and outcomes.

If the agent has strong stats:
- Large bold number with a label and one sentence of context
- Format as 3-4 stat callouts across the page

Example:
```
[X] Families Helped
Buyers and sellers across [city] who've trusted me with one of the
biggest financial decisions of their lives.

[X] Days Average on Market
My listings sell faster than the [city] market average of [X] days —
because pricing strategy and presentation matter.

[X]% of Asking Price
My sellers consistently close at or above asking price.
```

If the agent doesn't have polished stats, use their story instead:
Lead with the client story from Q6. 3-4 short paragraphs. Specific situation,
what the agent did, how it turned out. End with a one-line result.

---

### PAGE 5 — MY COMMITMENT TO YOU

The guarantees and promises page. What the client can hold the agent to.

If formal guarantees exist — explain them clearly with specific terms.

If no formal guarantees — write 3-4 commitment statements that are real and specific:

Example format:
```
I will always tell you the truth.
Even when it's not what you want to hear. If your home needs work before listing,
I'll tell you. If the price you want isn't supported by the market, I'll show you why.
My job is to help you make a great decision — not to tell you what sounds good.

I will respond within [X hours].
Real estate moves fast. I commit to responding to every call, text, and email
within [X hours] — because waiting on your agent shouldn't be part of the process.
```

Maximum 4 commitment statements. Each one must be specific enough to be held to.

---

### FINAL PAGE — LET'S TALK

Simple, warm closing page.

Short paragraph: "If anything in this guide resonates with you — whether you're
thinking about buying, selling, or just want to understand what's happening in
the [city] market — I'd love to connect."

Then:
- Booking link (large, prominent)
- Phone number
- Email
- Instagram / social handle
- "Know someone buying or selling in [city]? Share this with them."

---

## Document Design Notes (for .docx formatting)

- Cover page: agent name large and bold, city and year below, headshot placeholder centered
- Section headers: bold, in agent's brand color (from brand persona file)
- Offer items: bold title + regular body text, consistent spacing
- Stats page: large numbers (48pt+) in brand color, label in regular weight below
- Commitment statements: bold opening line + regular body text
- Footer on every page: agent name + city + booking link (small, consistent)
- Page breaks between each major section
- Clean margins — not cramped, not sparse
- Font: Arial or similar clean sans-serif throughout
- Brand colors: pull from brand persona file — fallback to black/dark gold/white
