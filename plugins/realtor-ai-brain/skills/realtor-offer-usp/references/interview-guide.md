# Interview Guide — Realtor Offer USP Skill

## How to Run This Interview

- 6 questions total. One at a time. Always.
- These are broad questions — let the agent talk. Don't cut them off.
- The goal is specifics. When something is vague, ask the follow-up.
- This should feel like 10 minutes with a business coach, not a questionnaire.
- If the brand persona file already answered part of a question, acknowledge it and
  ask only for what's missing. ("Your brand persona file mentions you focus on luxury
  sellers — for this question, I want to understand specifically what you offer them.")

---

## The 6 Questions

---

### Q1 — The Core Offer

**"When someone hires you as their agent — what do they actually get?
Walk me through everything you provide, start to finish."**

This is intentionally broad. Let them list everything. Capture it all.
Some agents will give a polished elevator pitch. Some will ramble and discover things
they'd never articulated before. Both are useful.

*Follow-up if surface-level:*
"That's a good start — let's go deeper. Beyond the standard things every agent does,
what do you do that most agents don't? Any services, perks, resources, or extras
you include that clients don't expect?"

*Follow-up if only one side of the transaction is covered:*
"That sounds like your buyer offer — what does your seller offer look like specifically?"
(or vice versa)

*Listen for and capture:*
- Specific services included (staging, photography, legal support, moving truck, etc.)
- Any vendor network or partnerships they offer access to
- Process-specific things (communication frequency, dedicated support, tools they use)
- Any financial perks (rebates, fee coverage, guarantees)
- Anything that removes friction, cost, or stress for the client

---

### Q2 — The Differentiator

**"Why should someone hire YOU specifically over every other agent in [city]?
What do you have or do that they genuinely can't get somewhere else?"**

This is the hardest question for most agents. Push past the first answer.

*Follow-up if "experience" or "hard work" or "great service":*
"Those are qualities — I'm looking for something more tangible. Is there a specific
area of [city] you know better than anyone? A particular type of client you've become
the go-to for? A result you consistently deliver that would be hard to match?"

*Follow-up if they mention a credential or certification:*
"Tell me more about that — what does having [credential] actually mean for a client
working with you? What can you do because of it that other agents can't?"

*Listen for and capture:*
- Genuine specialization (neighbourhood, property type, client type)
- Credentials, certifications, or training that translate to client benefit
- A network or resource no other agent has
- A track record or result they can back up with numbers
- A personal story or background that gives them unique insight

---

### Q3 — Proof and Results

**"What results do you consistently deliver for clients?
Any numbers, stats, or outcomes you're proud of?"**

Agents often undersell this. Help them think through it.

*Follow-up if they draw a blank:*
"Think about it this way — how many clients have you helped? What's your average
days on market for listings? Do your sellers typically get close to asking price?
Do your buyers usually win in competitive situations? Any of those numbers work."

*Follow-up if they have results but haven't framed them:*
"That's a strong result — how does that compare to the average agent in [city]
or the market average? Do you know?"

*Listen for and capture:*
- Years of experience and volume (homes sold, clients helped)
- Average days on market vs market average
- Sale-to-list price ratio for their listings
- Win rate in competitive offer situations
- Any awards, rankings, or recognition
- Specific client outcomes worth highlighting

---

### Q4 — The Process

**"What does working with you actually look like from start to finish?
Walk me through your process — what happens at each step?"**

This question reveals how organized and client-focused the agent is.
It also produces great content for the guidebook.

*Follow-up if vague:*
"Let's break it down — what happens on Day 1 when a new client signs with you?
Then what? What does the middle of the process look like? How do you handle the
final steps?"

*Listen for and capture:*
- First meeting / onboarding process
- How they communicate with clients (frequency, method)
- Key milestones they walk clients through
- How they handle negotiations
- What they do after closing (follow-up, ongoing relationship)
- Any tools, systems, or technology they use that benefit the client

---

### Q5 — Guarantees and Commitments

**"Do you make any specific promises or guarantees to clients?
What can someone count on you for — no matter what?"**

Not every agent has formal guarantees. That's fine. But most have informal ones
they've never articulated. This question brings those out.

*Follow-up if "I always do my best":*
"What's something a client could hold you accountable to? Something you'd say
to a prospect to give them confidence — like a response time promise, a
communication standard, a commitment to always be honest even if it's not
what they want to hear?"

*Follow-up if they have a formal guarantee:*
"Tell me exactly how that works — what are the terms?"

*Listen for and capture:*
- Response time commitments
- Communication promises
- Honesty / transparency commitments
- Any formal guarantees (sell your home or I'll buy it, etc.)
- What they do when something goes wrong

---

### Q6 — The Client Story

**"Tell me about a client you helped where you made a real difference —
not just closed a deal, but genuinely changed their outcome.
What happened?"**

This is the emotional anchor of the guidebook. One real story told well is worth
more than a list of bullet points. Let them tell it. Capture the details.

*Follow-up if the story is too short:*
"What was their situation when they came to you? What were they worried about?
What did you do that made the difference? How did it turn out?"

*Follow-up if they have trouble picking one:*
"Which client comes to mind first when you think about impact — not the biggest
deal, just the one where you know you really helped someone?"

*Listen for and capture:*
- The client's starting situation (what they were dealing with)
- What the challenge or risk was
- What the agent specifically did
- The outcome
- Any emotional element — relief, excitement, gratitude

---

### Q7 — Lead Magnets (free resources)

**"Last one — do you have any free guides, checklists, or resources you give people?
Like a first-time buyer guide, a seller's checklist, a relocation guide, or a monthly
market report? These are what your content points people to."**

Quick capture, not a deep dive. For each one, get: what it's called, who it's for, and
(if they want) a DM keyword (e.g. "BUYER", "SELLER", "RELOCATION"). This is what lets
every content skill say "DM me 'BUYER' for the guide" and point to something real.

*If they have none:* "No problem — that's worth building soon, even a simple one-pager.
For now I'll note you don't have one yet, so your content won't promise something that
doesn't exist." Capture "none yet" rather than inventing one.

---

## Handling Common Vague Answers

**"I provide full-service real estate"**
Response: "Help me understand what full-service means to you — if I listed out
everything included, what would be on that list?"

**"I'm very responsive and communicative"**
Response: "Is there a specific standard you hold yourself to — like a response
time guarantee, or a particular way you keep clients updated?"

**"I have a great network"**
Response: "Who specifically is in that network? Mortgage brokers, lawyers,
inspectors, stagers, contractors? What does having access to that network
actually save a client?"

**"I've been doing this for X years"**
Response: "What has X years taught you that a newer agent wouldn't know?
What do your clients get because of that experience?"

**"I really care about my clients"**
Response: "What does that look like in practice? What's something specific
you do because you care that a less invested agent might skip?"
