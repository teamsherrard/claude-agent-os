# Script Guide — Realtor Article Short-Form Talking Scripts

## The Core Concept

The agent films themselves reacting to a real news article as a local market expert.
The article is the credibility anchor. The agent's take is the value.

Viewers stop because the stat or headline grabs them.
They follow because the agent actually knows what it means for them locally.

---

## What Separates a Great Script from a Bad One

### The agent sounds like they already know this — not like they just read it

BAD: "I came across this article from CREB today that says inventory dropped by 18%..."
GOOD: "Calgary inventory just dropped 18% from last month. Here's exactly what that means if you're buying right now."

The article is visible on their screen or in the background. They don't need to cite it every sentence.

### The script is locally specific

National news without local context is useless. Every script must answer:
"What does this mean for someone buying or selling in [city] RIGHT NOW?"

BAD: "This is happening across the country and buyers everywhere should be aware."
GOOD: "In Calgary, what this means is that anything under $550K has less competition than it did six months ago. That's a window. Don't wait for it to close."

### The agent has an actual opinion

Agents who just repeat stats are forgettable. Agents who interpret them are followed.

Include one moment per script where the agent takes a position:
- "Here's what I'm telling every buyer who calls me right now..."
- "Most people read this headline as scary news. I see it differently."
- "Honestly? If you've been sitting on the fence, this changes the math."

### The script uses at least 2 specific details from the article

A script that could be written from the headline alone is not good enough.
Pull specific numbers, comparisons, dates, and findings from the article into the script.

BAD (headline-only): "Mortgage rates dropped again. This is good news for buyers. You should act now."

GOOD (article-content): "The Bank of Canada just held rates for the third meeting in a row — and fixed 5-year rates from major lenders have already moved down about 35 basis points since January. What that means in real numbers: on a $650K home, your monthly payment is roughly $180 lower than it was six months ago."

## Script Structure

### HOOK (3-5 seconds)
One punchy line. Stop the scroll immediately. Reference the stat, finding, or local market directly.
City name in the hook when it fits naturally. Word-for-word — agent reads this exactly.

**Formula options (pick one per script — all 5 in a batch must use different formulas):**
See hook-formulas.md for the full list.

### CONTEXT (5-10 seconds)
What happened, specifically. 1-2 sentences. Source name, number, city, timeframe.
No "according to this article" — deliver it like you already know this.

Example: "CREB just released their March stats. Sales are up 9% from last year, but here's the part nobody's talking about — inventory is still sitting 22% below the 10-year average."

### YOUR TAKE (25-40 seconds)
2-3 beats. This is the value. What does this mean for a buyer or seller in [city] right now?
Each beat should be something the agent would genuinely say to a client sitting across from them.
Opinionated. Specific. Locally relevant.

Beat structure options:
- "What this means for buyers is..." / "What this means for sellers is..."
- "Here's the part people miss..." / "Here's what I'm watching..."
- "My honest take is..." / "Here's what I'd tell you if you called me today..."

### CTA (5-10 seconds)
One action. Not three.

Default options:
- "Follow for [city] market updates every week"
- "Link in bio to book a free call"
- "DM me and I'll tell you exactly what this means for your situation"

Match to the agent's preferred CTA from the Brain (identity/voice.md) if available.

## Riff Notes

After every full script, write 4-6 bullet point riff notes for agents who prefer to speak freely
rather than read. These are shorthand talking points covering the same beats:

- Key stat to mention
- Local implication #1
- Local implication #2
- Agent's take / opinion
- CTA

## Script Length

| Target | Word Count | Runtime |
|--------|-----------|---------|
| Short | ~75 words | ~30 sec |
| Standard | ~110 words | ~45 sec |
| Long | ~150 words | ~60 sec |

Default: standard (45 seconds). Long enough to add value, short enough to hold attention.

---

## Script Quality Checklist

Before finalizing each script:
- [ ] Contains at least 2 specific details from the article (stat, number, comparison, named source)
- [ ] "You" or "your" appears at least 3 times
- [ ] City/market name appears at least once
- [ ] Agent takes a clear position at least once
- [ ] No "according to this article" language
- [ ] Hook is different from all other hooks in the batch
- [ ] Could NOT have been written from the headline alone

---

## Language to Never Use

- "According to this article..." / "I read that..." / "A recent report says..."
- "In today's ever-changing market..."
- "As your local real estate professional..."
- "Your dream home awaits..."
- "Reach out for all your real estate needs"
- "Now more than ever..."
- "The market is always a great time to buy"
- "I just wanted to share this..."
- "This is so exciting / incredible / amazing"

Every one of these phrases signals a generic agent with nothing real to say. Don't use them.

## Tone by Target Audience

Pull from the agent's brand personality file. Default guidance:

| Audience | Tone | Core message |
|----------|------|-------------|
| First-time buyers | Reassuring, educational | "Here's what this means and here's your move" |
| Move-up buyers | Confident, strategic | "This creates an opportunity — here's how to use it" |
| Sellers | Direct, data-driven | "Here's what this means for your asking price" |
| Investors | Analytical, specific | "Here's what this shift means for your numbers" |
| General local | Warm, community-expert | "Here's what's happening in our market right now" |
