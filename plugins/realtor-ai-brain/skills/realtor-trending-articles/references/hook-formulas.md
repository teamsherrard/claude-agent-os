# Hook Formulas — Real Estate Agent Article Videos

## The Rule

Every batch of 5 videos must use 5 different hook formulas.
After writing all 5 hooks, verify no two use the same pattern.
If two match — rewrite one.

Hooks must be under 10 words. They are read word-for-word.

---

## 10 Hook Formulas

### 1. The Stat Lead
Lead with the number. The number does the work.
Template: "[City] [stat] just [changed]. Here's what that means."
Example: "Calgary inventory just dropped to its lowest point in 3 years."
Best for: Market stats, price changes, rate announcements, inventory reports.

---

### 2. The Contrarian Take
Challenge what the viewer assumes. Creates instant curiosity.
Template: "Everyone thinks [X]. The data says something different."
Example: "Everyone thinks this is a bad time to buy. The numbers disagree."
Best for: Market outlook articles, rate news, affordability stories where the real answer surprises people.

---

### 3. The Direct Address
Speak directly to one type of viewer. Makes them feel called out (in a good way).
Template: "If you're [specific viewer] in [city], stop scrolling."
Example: "If you're thinking about selling in Calgary this spring, you need to see this."
Best for: Seller-specific news, buyer opportunity articles, market shift stories.

---

### 4. The Window
Frame the moment as an opportunity that won't last.
Template: "There's a window open right now in [city]. Here's what it is."
Example: "There's a window open for [city] buyers right now that wasn't there six months ago."
Best for: Rate drops, inventory increases, market softening stories — any time the conditions favour action.

---

### 5. The Myth Buster
Name a common belief and bust it with the article's finding.
Template: "Most [buyers/sellers] believe [X]. This data shows that's not true."
Example: "Most [city] buyers think they need 20% down. That hasn't been true for a while."
Best for: First-time buyer content, affordability stories, misconception-correcting articles.

---

### 6. The Local Lens
Put the city front and center. Hyper-local hooks outperform generic ones.
Template: "[City] just [specific event]. Here's what it actually means."
Example: "The [city] real estate board just released their [month] numbers. One stat stood out."
Best for: Board stats releases, local policy news, city-specific market updates.

---

### 7. The Before/After
Set up the contrast. What changed and why it matters.
Template: "[X] months ago [city] looked like [A]. Today it looks like [B]."
Example: "Six months ago [city] had 3,000 active listings. Today it has under 1,800."
Best for: Year-over-year comparisons, market shift articles, trend stories.

---

### 8. The Question
A direct question to the viewer that makes them want the answer.
Template: "Are you [doing the thing / thinking the thing] in [city]? Watch this first."
Example: "Are you waiting for rates to drop before buying in [city]? Watch this first."
Best for: Rate articles, timing the market content, decision-making stories.

---

### 9. The Honest Take
Signal that what follows is the agent's real opinion — not a sales pitch.
Template: "My honest take on [what just happened in city]."
Example: "My honest take on what the Bank of Canada just did to your mortgage."
Best for: Rate decisions, market forecast articles, policy changes — anything where the agent's interpretation is the value.

---

### 10. The Specific Number
Lead with a very specific number that feels too precise to ignore.
Template: "[Specific number] — that's [what it represents] in [city] right now."
Example: "18 days. That's the average time a home sits on market in [city] right now."
Best for: Days on market stats, specific price data, inventory numbers, affordability metrics.

---

## Hook Selection Guide

| Article Type | Best Hook Formulas |
|---|---|
| Board stats release | Stat Lead, Local Lens, Specific Number |
| Rate announcement | Honest Take, Question, Window |
| Inventory/supply news | Before/After, Window, Stat Lead |
| Buyer affordability | Myth Buster, Question, Direct Address |
| Market forecast | Contrarian Take, Honest Take, Before/After |
| Local development | Local Lens, Direct Address, Window |
| Seller market update | Direct Address, Local Lens, Stat Lead |

---

## Hook Quality Test

Before using a hook, ask:
- Is it under 10 words? (If not, trim it)
- Does it name the city or reference the local market? (Preferred but not required for every hook)
- Would someone mid-scroll stop to find out the rest? (If not, rewrite)
- Is it different from every other hook in the batch? (Must be yes)
- Does it avoid hype words like "insane," "game-changer," "unbelievable"? (Must be yes)
