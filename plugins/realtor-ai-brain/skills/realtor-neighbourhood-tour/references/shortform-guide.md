# Short-Form Guide — Neighbourhood Tour Reels & TikToks

## The Core Rule

Every short-form concept pulled from the tour must work as a completely standalone
piece of content. A viewer who has never seen the YouTube video should get full value
from each Reel/TikTok on its own.

Never say "watch my full YouTube video for more" as the hook. Never reference the
longer video in the opening. If the short-form is good enough, viewers will find
the long-form themselves.

---

## Concept 1 — The Stat Hook

**What it is:**
A 30-45 second video built entirely around the single most compelling number from
the neighbourhood research. The stat does the stopping. The agent explains what it means.

**Which stop to film at:**
The stop that best illustrates the stat visually. If it's a price stat — a residential
street. If it's a school stat — outside the school. If it's a walkability stat —
on the main commercial strip.

**Script structure:**
```
HOOK (word-for-word, 3-5 seconds):
"[Specific stat about this neighbourhood] — and most people have no idea."

CONTEXT (5-10 seconds):
[What the stat means, without jargon. One or two sentences.]

LOCAL TAKE (15-20 seconds):
[What this means specifically for the target buyer in this city right now.
The agent's opinion. Specific to this neighbourhood.]

CTA (5-10 seconds):
[Single action — follow, link in bio, or "DM me [neighbourhood] and I'll
send you everything I know about this area"]
```

**Caption:** Opens with the hook stat adapted for reading. 2-3 lines of context.
CTA. 8-10 hashtags including at least 2 local (#[City]RealEstate, #[Neighbourhood]).

## Concept 2 — The Local Gem

**What it is:**
A 30-45 second video filmed at the most interesting local spot discovered in research —
a standout restaurant, café, park, or hidden feature of the neighbourhood. The agent
talks about why it matters to someone considering living there.

The angle is: "this is what living here actually feels like" — not a real estate pitch,
a lifestyle moment. This is the most shareable concept of the three because it doesn't
feel like real estate content.

**Which stop to film at:**
The specific restaurant, café, park, or local spot being featured. Agent films
themselves there — not just talking about it, but visibly at the location.

**Script structure:**
```
HOOK (word-for-word, 3-5 seconds):
"[Neighbourhood name] has [local gem] — and this alone tells you everything about
what kind of community this is."
OR
"If you want to know what living in [Neighbourhood] actually feels like — come here."

CONTEXT (5-10 seconds):
[What the spot is, how long it's been there, what it's known for.
One or two sentences. Specific — not "a great local café."]

THE POINT (15-20 seconds):
[Why this matters to a buyer. What does this spot signal about the neighbourhood?
What kind of person is going to love living here because of this?]

CTA (5-10 seconds):
[Single action]
```

**Caption:** Opens with the hook. Names the specific spot. 2-3 lines connecting it
to the lifestyle of living in this neighbourhood. CTA. Hashtags.

## Concept 3 — The Buyer Take

**What it is:**
Pure talking head. No background information needed. The agent gives their direct,
honest advice to the target buyer type about this neighbourhood. Their opinion,
their take, their recommendation.

This is the most personal format and builds the strongest trust. It positions the
agent as an expert who actually knows this neighbourhood — not just someone reading
stats off a page.

**Which stop to film at:**
The most visually compelling stop — ideally somewhere that shows the neighbourhood's
character clearly behind the agent. A nice residential street, the main commercial
strip, the park — somewhere that looks like the neighbourhood, not a parking lot.

**Script structure:**
```
HOOK (word-for-word, 3-5 seconds):
"If I were a [target buyer type] looking in [City] right now — here's what I'd
want to know about [Neighbourhood] before I made a decision."

TAKE 1 (10 seconds):
[The strongest thing going for this neighbourhood for this buyer type]

TAKE 2 (10 seconds):
[The honest trade-off or thing to be aware of — builds credibility]

TAKE 3 (10 seconds):
[The agent's overall verdict — who this neighbourhood is right for and who it isn't]

CTA (5-10 seconds):
[Single action]
```

**Caption:** Opens with the hook adapted for reading. Personal and opinionated tone.
No "in today's market" language. CTA. Hashtags.

---

## Cross-Concept Rules

- [ ] All 3 hooks use different formulas
- [ ] All 3 are filmed at different stops — no two at the same location
- [ ] Captions for all 3 have completely different opening lines
- [ ] No two concepts cover the same primary stat or talking point
- [ ] The 3 videos together give a buyer a fuller picture of the neighbourhood
      than any one alone
- [ ] None of the 3 reference "the full YouTube video" in their hook or body
