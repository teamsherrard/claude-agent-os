# Funnel Guide — the opt-in page that converts

One page, one job: **get the visitor to opt into the lead magnet** (name + email). It's a *capture* page, not
a sales page — short, focused, trust-building. Every section answers the reader's next question and pushes
toward the opt-in. (Read `${CLAUDE_PLUGIN_ROOT}/shared/copywriting-kb.md` for how to write each line.)

## The flow (why this order works)
```
HERO     →  "What is this and is it for me?"          promise + who + CTA
ABOUT    →  "Why should I trust this person?"          expertise + wins + process
OFFER    →  "What's it like to work with them?"        transformation + why them + proof
OPT-IN   →  "Okay — what do I get, and how?"           the magnet + name/email + CTA
```
Each section earns the next scroll. By the opt-in, the reader trusts the agent and wants the guide — so
handing over an email feels easy. Keep momentum: short blocks, sub-headers, bullets, white space.

---

## SECTION 1 — HERO (90% of the result)
The first screen. If it's vague, they leave. Three parts:
- **Headline** — the magnet's core promise, benefit-led, for this audience in this market. Clear over clever.
  *"The First-Time Buyer's Guide to Winning in Calgary's New-Build Market."*
- **Subhead** (1–2 lines) — expand the promise, name who it's for, remove doubt.
  *"A plain-English walkthrough of every step, incentive, and number — from a Calgary new-build specialist
  who tells you the truth, not a sales pitch."*
- **CTA button** — first-person, specific: *"Send me the free guide."*
The headline MUST equal the magnet's promise (alignment — house rules #7).

## SECTION 2 — ABOUT THE AGENT (earns the trust to opt in)
People don't give a stranger their email. Three beats, every one framed back to the reader:
- **What makes them amazing** — niche + why it matters *to this reader*. Specific expertise beats generic
  "top agent." *"I only do new builds in Calgary, so I know which builders cut corners and which incentives
  are actually worth asking for — which means you don't find out the hard way."*
- **Their wins** — real proof from `proof.md`: numbers, results, a short testimonial. Concrete > adjectives.
  If proof is thin, lean on specific process and real experience; never invent a result.
- **Their process** — the simple "here's how I make this easy," 3 steps max. Reassurance: there's a clear
  path and the agent has walked it many times.
Tone: humble-confident, human, local.

## SECTION 3 — THE OFFER (makes the agent the obvious choice)
Present `offer.md` as an outcome, never a task list:
- **The transformation** — where the reader is now (worried, unsure) → where the agent gets them (confident,
  informed, in the right home at the right price).
- **Why this agent** — the USP tied to the reader's specific fear (overpaying, mispricing, the unknown).
- **Proof** — one more concrete result or testimonial.
- **Compliance-safe** — describe the experience and approach; never guarantee a price, saving, or sale.
By the end the reader is warm — so grabbing the free guide is the natural next step.

## SECTION 4 — THE OPT-IN (the ask)
Make grabbing the magnet a no-brainer:
- **Name the magnet** + a one-line promise.
- **"What you'll get" — 3–5 bullets** of concrete value. These MUST match the guide's actual pages
  (alignment — house rules #7). Specificity is what sells the opt-in.
- **The form** — name + email only. Nothing else (every extra field costs leads).
- **Reassurance** by the button — *"Free. Instant. No spam — unsubscribe anytime."*
- **CTA button** — same promise as the hero: *"Send me the free guide."*
- **Thank-you state** (after submit) — warm confirmation + *"check your inbox, your guide's on the way,"* in
  the agent's voice. **No call booking** — the follow-up is the agent's job (house rules #4).

## What converts vs. what kills it
**Converts:** one clear promise · trust built before the ask · real local proof · benefit-led "you" copy ·
a tiny, reassured form · one repeated CTA · short, skimmable, lots of white space.
**Kills it:** a vague headline · "me/we" bragging · hype and fake urgency · a long form · multiple competing
CTAs · a wall of text · any claim the magnet doesn't pay off · adding a "book a call" step.

## Fair-Housing + honesty note
Keep every neighbourhood/market reference factual (price, schools by name, commute, amenities) — never "good
area" / "family-friendly" proxies or who "belongs" somewhere. Real proof only — no invented testimonials or
stats, no guarantees. Run the whole page through `~/realtor-brain/identity/compliance.md`.
