# Funnel Guide — the opt-in page that converts

One page, one job: **get the visitor to opt into the lead magnet** (name + email). It's a **value-led capture
page** — it EARNS the opt-in with genuine local authority and a real offer, never hype. Trust-building,
skimmable, focused on the opt-in as the one action. (Read `${CLAUDE_PLUGIN_ROOT}/shared/copywriting-kb.md` for
how to write each line.)

## The flow (why this order works)
```
HERO       →  "What is this and is it for me?"          promise + who + CTA
ABOUT      →  "Why should I trust this person?"          expertise + wins + process
MARKET     →  "Do they really know MY area?"             real communities + local depth (+ photos)
WHY ME     →  "What do they offer + why hire them?"      full offer + USP + why-work-with-them
OPT-IN     →  "Okay — what do I get, and how?"           the magnet + name/email + CTA
```
Each section earns the next scroll. By the opt-in, the reader trusts the agent, sees they truly know the local
market, knows exactly why they'd hire them — and wants the guide. Keep momentum: short blocks, sub-headers,
bullets, white space. **A thin page reads salesy; this depth is what makes it read like value.**

## SECTION 1 — HERO (90% of the result)
The first screen. If it's vague, they leave. Three parts:
- **Headline** — the magnet's core promise, benefit-led, for this audience in this market. Clear over clever.
  *"The First-Time Buyer's Guide to Winning in Calgary's New-Build Market."*
- **Subhead** (1–2 lines) — expand the promise, name who it's for, remove doubt.
  *"A plain-English walkthrough of every step, incentive, and number — from a Calgary new-build specialist
  who tells you the truth, not a sales pitch."*
- **CTA button** — first-person, specific: *"Send me the free guide."*
The headline MUST equal the magnet's promise (alignment — house rules #7).

## SECTION 2 — ABOUT THE AGENT (earns the trust to opt in)
People don't give a stranger their email. Three beats, every one framed back to the reader:
- **What makes them amazing** — niche + why it matters *to this reader*. Specific expertise beats generic
  "top agent." *"I only do new builds in Calgary, so I know which builders cut corners and which incentives
  are actually worth asking for — which means you don't find out the hard way."*
- **Their wins** — real proof from `proof.md`: numbers, results, a short testimonial. Concrete > adjectives.
  If proof is thin, lean on specific process and real experience; never invent a result.
- **Their process** — the simple "here's how I make this easy," 3 steps max. Reassurance: there's a clear
  path and the agent has walked it many times.
Tone: humble-confident, human, local.

## SECTION 3 — THE LOCAL MARKET ("your communities") — the un-fakeable section
This is what a generic page can NEVER produce, and what flips the whole page from "salesy" to "valuable."
Pull the agent's real market from `market.md`:
- **The communities they serve, by name** — the specific areas/neighbourhoods (e.g. the new-build communities
  they specialize in). Show genuine local depth in FACTS — price bands, what's being built, schools by name,
  commute, amenities. Never "good area" / "family-friendly" proxies (Fair Housing).
- **Why their local knowledge matters to the reader** — "the deep-south new-build communities I know inside
  out" → so you don't overpay or pick the wrong one. Tie the depth back to the reader's benefit.
- **Real photos** — cue the agent to upload their own community/area shots in claude.ai/design (Phase 3 of the
  skill). Real places only.
Pure value + authority — it's the strongest answer to "why not just Google it / use ChatGPT."

## SECTION 4 — WHY WORK WITH [AGENT] (the offer + USP — the section most pages are missing)
The real "why hire me," straight from `offer.md` — NOT just the guide's process. Make it substantive:
- **What they actually offer** — their real services / how they work with clients (buyer representation, the
  new-build process, negotiation — whatever the offer is). Present it as outcomes, not a task list.
- **Their USP** — what they do differently that no other agent does, tied to the reader's fear (overpaying,
  mispricing, the unknown). The heart of "why them."
- **The transformation** — where the reader is now (worried, unsure) → where the agent gets them (confident,
  informed, in the right home at the right price).
- **Proof** — one more concrete result or testimonial from `proof.md`.
- **Compliance-safe** — describe the experience and approach; never guarantee a price, saving, or sale.
By the end the reader trusts the agent AND knows exactly why they'd hire them — so the opt-in is the easy next step.

## SECTION 5 — THE OPT-IN (the ask)
Make grabbing the magnet a no-brainer:
- **Name the magnet** + a one-line promise.
- **"What you'll get" — 3–5 bullets** of concrete value. These MUST match the guide's actual pages
  (alignment — house rules #7). Specificity is what sells the opt-in.
- **The form** — name + email only. Nothing else (every extra field costs leads).
- **Reassurance** by the button — *"Free. Instant. No spam — unsubscribe anytime."*
- **CTA button** — same promise as the hero: *"Send me the free guide."*
- **Thank-you state** (after submit) — warm confirmation + *"check your inbox, your guide's on the way,"* in
  the agent's voice. **No call booking** — the follow-up is the agent's job (house rules #4).

## What converts vs. what kills it
**Converts:** one clear promise · trust built before the ask · **real local-market depth (named communities)** ·
**a substantive offer + USP (the real why-hire-them)** · real proof · benefit-led "you" copy · a tiny, reassured
form · one repeated CTA · short, skimmable, lots of white space.
**Kills it:** a vague headline · **a thin, all-pitch page with no genuine value (reads salesy)** · "me/we"
bragging · hype and fake urgency · a long form · multiple competing CTAs · a wall of text · any claim the
magnet doesn't pay off · adding a "book a call" step.

## Fair-Housing + honesty note
Keep every neighbourhood/market reference factual (price, schools by name, commute, amenities) — never "good
area" / "family-friendly" proxies or who "belongs" somewhere. Real proof only — no invented testimonials or
stats, no guarantees. Run the whole page through `~/realtor-brain/identity/compliance.md`.
