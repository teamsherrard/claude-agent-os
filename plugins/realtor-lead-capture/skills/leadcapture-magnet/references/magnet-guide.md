# Lead Magnet Guide — what to build and why it works

A lead magnet earns the email. It works when it solves **one real problem** for **a clearly defined reader** —
one avatar for a niche guide, or the whole audience under one brand-level promise for a brand-led guide —
fast, and leaves them thinking "if the free thing is this good, I want this agent." Build from the offer.

## The two magnet shapes (SKILL.md Phase 1 picks one)
- **A. Brand-led (general) — the default for most agents.** ONE comprehensive guide for the agent's **whole
  audience and full offer suite** (buyers AND sellers), built around their personal brand. Core promise
  example: *"Everything you need to buy or sell in [city] with confidence."* It still carries **ONE clear
  brand-level promise** — that promise becomes the funnel headline.
- **B. Audience-specific (niche) — when the agent has one dominant focus.** A focused guide matched to their
  strongest avatar + offer — pick one type from the table below.

## Audience-specific magnet types (shape B — pick one audience)
| Type | Core promise example |
|---|---|
| First-time buyer guide | "Everything a first-time [city] buyer needs — from pre-approval to keys, in plain English." |
| New-build buyer guide | "How to buy a new build in [city] without overpaying — incentives, builders, and the fine print." |
| Relocation buyer guide | "Moving to [city]? The honest guide to neighbourhoods, prices, and what to know before you land." |
| Seller's pricing/prep guide | "What your [city] home is really worth — and the prep that adds the most before you list." |
| Downsizer / move-up seller guide | "Selling and buying at once in [city]: how to time it without owning two homes." |
| Investor guide | "Cash-flow vs. appreciation in [city]: where the numbers actually work right now." |

For a niche magnet, pick the type that matches the agent's **offer + strongest avatar** — it speaks to one
person. A brand-led magnet speaks to the whole audience but still carries ONE clear brand-level promise.
Either way, one promise is what lets the funnel's copy land.

## What makes a magnet worth opting in for
- **Solves one specific, real problem** the reader is actively worried about (overpaying, mispricing, the
  unknown of a new city). Not "10 tips" — *the* answer to *their* question.
- **Genuinely useful — never a tease.** Give the real content. A magnet that's all hype and no substance
  burns trust (and the funnel's promise). Deliver value worth far more than an email.
- **Local and specific.** Real neighbourhoods, real price bands, real steps, real numbers. This is the
  agent's local authority on a page — the thing a generic download can't fake.
- **Quick to consume.** A focused guide (5–9 short pages) beats a 40-page doc nobody finishes.
- **Naturally leads to the agent.** Because it's built from the offer, the obvious next step *is* working
  with them — without a single pushy line.

## Guide structure (5–9 pages)
1. **Cover / the promise** — title + one-line promise + who it's for. (Becomes the funnel headline.)
2. **The framing page** — name the problem and the stakes, and promise the way through. Short.
3. **The value pages (the body)** — the actual content, one clear idea per page: the steps, the checklist,
   the numbers, the local specifics, the mistakes to avoid. This is the meat — make it real.
4. **The "what good looks like" page** — a brief, concrete example or mini case (real proof if available).
5. **How [agent] helps next** — soft close in their voice: where they are now → the agent makes it easy →
   where to reach them. **No call booking** — a warm "reach out anytime / here's where to find me."

## Tie it to the offer (this is the point)
The magnet and the offer are the same story at two stages: the magnet solves the first problem for free; the
offer is the full solution. Build the magnet so the reader finishes it already wanting what the agent sells —
then the funnel just makes it easy to raise their hand.

## Honesty + compliance
Real numbers and real proof only — never invent a stat, an incentive, or a testimonial. No guarantees of
price/savings/sale. Fair-Housing-safe (facts, not "good area" proxies). Run it through `compliance.md`.
