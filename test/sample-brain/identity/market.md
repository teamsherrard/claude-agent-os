# Jordan — Market & Geography
*identity · the city/community names skills must use specifically — never "locally" or "your area"*

**Primary market:** Calgary, Alberta (with focus on the NW quadrant)

**Communities / neighbourhoods served:**
- Tuscany
- Bowness
- Rocky Ridge
- Arbour Lake
- Cochrane (just NW of the city)

**Price ranges:**
- Primary: $400,000 – $750,000
- Secondary: $750,000+ (move-up detached)

**Top neighbourhoods buyers ask about:**
- Tuscany — family-friendly, lake access, top schools
- Arbour Lake — only NW lake community, strong resale
- Bowness — character homes + redevelopment upside near the river

**Underrated / up-and-coming area:** Bowness — older area being revitalized, still affordable entry to NW.

**Why people move TO this market:** Affordability vs Vancouver/Toronto, no provincial sales tax, jobs, space — lots of out-of-province relocations.
**Why people leave:** Job relocation, retirement to BC interior, cashing out detached equity.
**Common misconceptions about this market:** That Calgary is "just oil" and prices are flat — NW family communities have been competitive with multiple offers.

## Local market intelligence
- **What buyers consistently get wrong here:** They underestimate how fast well-priced detached homes under $700K move in NW.
- **The real reason buyers/sellers hesitate:** Relocating buyers fear "buying in the wrong community" sight-unseen; sellers fear listing before they've found their next place.
- **What Jordan knows that most agents don't:** NW micro-markets behave very differently — Tuscany vs Bowness can move in opposite directions in the same month.
